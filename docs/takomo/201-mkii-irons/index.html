About the Takomo 201 MKII

The Iron 201 MKII replaced Takomo's forged 201 on 7 January 2026 and changes the model's character: where the original 201 was a one-piece S20C forging, the MKII is a cast 17-4 hollow body with a machined variable-thickness face plate in the 4- through 7-irons and a flat cast face in the 8-iron through pitching wedge. It is positioned as a players-distance iron with a shorter blade length, thinner sole and trimmed topline compared with the 101 MKII. The 7-iron is 31 degrees and the pitching wedge 46 degrees, and the stock set is 4-iron through pitching wedge. Offsets below are converted from Takomo's published millimetre figures.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Takomo 201 MKII 7-iron?

The Takomo 201 MKII 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and 2.77 mm of offset.

Are the Takomo 201 MKII irons forged?

No. Unlike the forged S20C 201 it replaces, the 201 MKII is a cast 17-4 stainless hollow body with a machined 17-4 variable-thickness face plate in the 4- through 7-irons.

What clubs come in a Takomo 201 MKII set?

Seven clubs, 4-iron through pitching wedge, with a 46-degree pitching wedge.

Where it sits in the lineup

Introduced in 2026, the Takomo 201 MKII is a current players distance set in the Takomo range, with no replacement announced. It replaced the Takomo 201. New sets are built to the standard lofts, lies and lengths in the chart above, though a fitting can move any of them — so confirm the numbers on the order sheet. Used sets appear on eBay, 2nd Swing and Golf Avenue, and should be measured against this chart before buying.
