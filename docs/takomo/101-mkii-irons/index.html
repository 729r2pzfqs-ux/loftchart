About the Takomo 101 MKII

The Iron 101 MKII is the second-generation Takomo 101, released on 7 January 2026 alongside the 201 MKII and 201T MKII. It keeps the hollow-body game-improvement formula but adds a variable-face- thickness plate in the 5- through 8-irons, a flat cast face in the 9-iron through gap wedge, and a reworked perimeter weighting system. The stock set is seven clubs, 5-iron through gap wedge, rather than the 4-PW makeup of the original 101. Lofts are strong, with a 29-degree 7-iron, a 43-degree pitching wedge and a 48-degree gap wedge. Offsets below are converted from Takomo's published millimetre figures.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Takomo 101 MKII 7-iron?

The Takomo 101 MKII 7-iron has a loft of 29 degrees, with a 62.5-degree lie and 3.4 mm of offset.

What clubs come in a Takomo 101 MKII set?

Seven clubs, 5-iron through gap wedge. The pitching wedge is 43 degrees and the gap wedge is 48 degrees.

How is the 101 MKII different from the original Takomo 101?

The MKII adds a variable-thickness face in the 5- through 8-irons, a two-tone finish and a thinner topline, changes the set makeup from 4-PW to 5-GW, and runs one degree weaker at the 7-iron (29 versus 30 degrees).

Finding a used set

The Takomo 101 MKII is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
