About the Takomo 201

The Iron 201 is Takomo's forged players cavity back, one-piece forged from soft S20C carbon steel with a precision-milled face and back cavity. It is the brand's value forged set, sitting between the cast 101 family and the tour-shaped 301 CB and MB. Lofts match the 101T at a 32-degree 7-iron and a 46-degree pitching wedge, but lie angles are half a degree flatter through the set. The stock set is seven clubs, 4-iron through pitching wedge, with KBS Tour steel shafts and Lamkin Crossline grips. The 201 arrived in autumn 2021, not 2023 as Takomo's current brand timeline implies - it was on sale on the company's original WooCommerce site by October 2021, a few months after the 101. Takomo revised the set once, very early in its life. The launch table archived in October 2021 listed lofts of 22/24/27/31/35/39/44 with slightly different head weights; by January 2022 the table had settled on the 22/25/28/32/36/41/46 lofts shown below, and those have been unchanged from then through the current listing. Lie angles and the forged S20C construction were the same in both tables. The specs here are the settled 2022-onward set, which covers all but the first few months of production.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Takomo 201 7-iron?

The Takomo 201 7-iron has a loft of 32 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

Are the Takomo 201 irons forged?

Yes. The 201 is a one-piece forging in soft S20C carbon steel, with the face and back cavity precision-milled after forging.

What clubs come in a Takomo 201 set?

Seven clubs, 4-iron through pitching wedge. The pitching wedge is 46 degrees.

When did the Takomo 201 come out?

Autumn 2021. Takomo's own site listed the 201 for sale in October 2021, a few months after the 101, although the brand's current timeline dates it to 2023. The set was re-specced once shortly after launch: the original listing showed a 31-degree 7-iron and a 44-degree pitching wedge, and by January 2022 those had become the 32 and 46 degrees the set has carried ever since.

Buying a set

Takomo has since introduced the Takomo 201 MKII above it in the range, so the 201 is at the end of its retail life rather than fully retired — closeout sets still turn up at retailers while stock lasts, and it is well represented used on eBay, 2nd Swing and Golf Avenue. Check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying a used set: irons are routinely bent from standard during a fitting.
