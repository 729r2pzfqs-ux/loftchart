About the Takomo 201T MKII

The Iron 201T MKII is a new model in Takomo's January 2026 MKII launch and the brand's most compact hollow-body iron. It uses a cast 17-4 body with a machined variable-thickness face plate and a tungsten weight bar in the 4- through 7-irons, switching to a flat cast face and steel weighting in the 8-iron through pitching wedge. Against the 201 MKII it has a trimmed topline, a thinner tapered sole, a shorter blade length, roughly 0.3 mm less offset and lofts two degrees weaker at the 7-iron. The 7-iron is 33 degrees and the pitching wedge 47 degrees, in a 4-iron through pitching wedge set. Offsets below are converted from Takomo's published millimetre figures.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Takomo 201T MKII 7-iron?

The Takomo 201T MKII 7-iron has a loft of 33 degrees, with a 62.5-degree lie and 2.46 mm of offset.

How does the 201T MKII differ from the 201 MKII?

The 201T MKII has a shorter blade length, thinner sole and topline, about 0.3 mm less offset and a tungsten weight bar in the long irons. Its lofts are two degrees weaker at the 7-iron (33 versus 31 degrees).

What clubs come in a Takomo 201T MKII set?

Seven clubs, 4-iron through pitching wedge, with a 47-degree pitching wedge.

Finding a used set

The Takomo 201T MKII is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
