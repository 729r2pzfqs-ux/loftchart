About the Titleist DCI 981

The DCI 981 is the 1998 perimeter-weighted cast iron that carried the DCI name into its most widely played era. It is the most heavily offset set in the DCI family, running .260 inches at the 1-iron and still .125 inches at the 9-iron, which is what gives it its high, straight ball flight. Lofts are traditional by modern standards with a 35 degree 7-iron, and the stock set ran unusually deep: a full 1-iron through lob wedge was catalogued, with the scoring clubs built heavier at D3 and D5 swing weight.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist DCI 981 7-iron?

The Titleist DCI 981 7-iron is 35 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 36.75 inch standard steel length.

How many clubs were in the Titleist DCI 981 set?

Titleist catalogued the DCI 981 from a 1-iron through a lob wedge, thirteen clubs in all, though most retail sets were sold as 3-PW.

Why does the DCI 981 have so much offset?

The DCI 981 was designed as a game-improvement iron and carries .260 inches of offset at the 1-iron, tapering to .040 inches at the wedges, which helps square the face and raise launch.

Finding a used set

The Titleist DCI 981 ran from 1998 to 2001 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
