About the Titleist DCI 762

The DCI 762 is the 2001 dual-cavity iron that replaced the DCI 990 at the game-improvement end of the Titleist range. It is investment cast in 17-4 stainless with a dual-cavity back that pushes weight low and to the perimeter, and it runs noticeably stronger than the 990 it replaced: the 7-iron is 34 degrees against 36, and the set carries a half-degree 3-iron loft of 21.5 degrees. Offset tapers from .180 inches at the 1-iron to .099 inches at the gap wedge, and every club is built to D2 swing weight.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist DCI 762 7-iron?

The Titleist DCI 762 7-iron is 34 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

How do the DCI 762 lofts compare with the DCI 990?

The DCI 762 is two degrees stronger through the middle of the set: its 7-iron is 34 degrees against 36 degrees on the DCI 990, and its pitching wedge is 46 degrees against 49.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Titleist DCI 762?

The DCI 762 pitching wedge is 46 degrees, and the matching gap wedge, badged W, is 50 degrees.

Finding a used set

The Titleist DCI 762 ran from 2001 to 2003 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
