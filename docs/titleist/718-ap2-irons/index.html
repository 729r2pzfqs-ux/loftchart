About the Titleist 718 AP2

The 718 AP2 is the sixth and final AP2 before the line became the T-Series. It retains the co-forged 1025 carbon steel construction with high-density tungsten in the heel and toe, and carries the same lofts as the 714 and 716 before it: a 34 degree 7-iron and a 46 degree pitching wedge across a 3-iron to 50 degree gap wedge set. Titleist published the 718 offsets in millimetres rather than inches, running 4.1mm at the 3-iron down to 1.6mm at the gap wedge. Stock steel was the ascending-mass True Temper AMT Tour White.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist 718 AP2 7-iron?

The Titleist 718 AP2 7-iron is 34 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What replaced the Titleist 718 AP2?

The T100, launched in 2019, replaced the 718 AP2 as Titleist's tour players iron and carried the same 34 degree 7-iron loft.

What is the offset on the Titleist 718 AP2?

Titleist published 718 offsets in millimetres: 4.1mm at the 3-iron tapering to 1.6mm at the gap wedge, which is roughly .161 to .063 inches.

Finding a used set

The Titleist 718 AP2 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
