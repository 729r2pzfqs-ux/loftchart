About the Titleist T200

The T200 is the players-distance iron of Titleist's original 2019 T-Series, replacing the 718 AP3. It uses a hollow-body construction with a forged SUP-10 face and Max Impact internal support that stiffens the centre of the face, plus high-density tungsten around the perimeter. Lofts sit four degrees stronger than the T100 through the middle of the set, giving a 30 degree 7-iron and a 43 degree pitching wedge, while keeping a compact players-style head. Titleist did not publish offset figures for this generation.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist T200 7-iron?

The 2019 Titleist T200 7-iron is 30 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

How do the 2019 and 2023 Titleist T200 differ?

The 2023 T200 is half a degree weaker in the middle irons, with a 30.5 degree 7-iron against 30, and its long-iron lies are half a degree more upright.

What is the difference between the Titleist T100 and T200?

The T200 is the distance model: its 7-iron is 30 degrees against the T100's 34, and it uses a hollow forged-face construction rather than a solid forged cavity back.

Finding a used set

The Titleist T200 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
