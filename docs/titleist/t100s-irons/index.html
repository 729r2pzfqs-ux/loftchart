About the Titleist T100S

The T100S is the strong-lofted variant of the T100, built on the same forged 1025 carbon steel head with high-density tungsten weighting but set two degrees stronger through the whole set. That gives a 32 degree 7-iron against the T100's 34, and a 44 degree pitching wedge against 46. Lies, lengths and offsets are identical to the T100, so the S is purely a loft play: the same tour-compact head delivering a lower, longer ball flight. It was replaced by the T150 in 2023.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist T100S 7-iron?

The Titleist T100S 7-iron is 32 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the difference between the Titleist T100 and T100S?

The T100S is two degrees stronger on every club. Its 7-iron is 32 degrees against 34 on the T100, and its pitching wedge is 44 degrees against 46. Lie, length and offset are identical.

What replaced the Titleist T100S?

The T150, launched in 2023, replaced the T100S and carries the same 32 degree 7-iron and 44 degree pitching wedge.

Finding a used set

The Titleist T100S is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
