About the Titleist 718 CB

The 718 CB is the compact forged cavity back of Titleist's 2018 range, sitting between the 718 AP2 and the 718 MB. Lofts are unchanged from the 716 CB, keeping a 35 degree 7-iron and a 47 degree pitching wedge across a 2-iron to pitching wedge set, and the refined leading edge is the main design change. High-density tungsten in the long irons keeps the moment of inertia up in a head that is visibly smaller than the AP2. It was the last CB to use the Titleist year-number naming before the 620 CB.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist 718 CB 7-iron?

The Titleist 718 CB 7-iron is 35 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

Do the 716 CB and 718 CB have the same lofts?

Yes. Both run 18 degrees at the 2-iron through 47 degrees at the pitching wedge, with a 35 degree 7-iron. The 718 CB is half a degree more upright at the 2-iron.

How does the 718 CB differ from the 718 AP2?

The CB is a smaller, less offset head and is one degree weaker through the middle of the set: a 35 degree 7-iron against 34, and a 47 degree pitching wedge against 46.

Finding a used set

The Titleist 718 CB ran from 2017 to 2019 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
