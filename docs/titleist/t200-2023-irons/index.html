About the Titleist T200 (2023)

The second-generation T200 arrived in 2023 with a slimmer profile and a reworked hollow-body build, keeping the forged face and Max Impact internal support of the original but tightening the shaping toward the T150. Lofts move half a degree weaker through the middle irons compared with the 2019 T200, giving a 30.5 degree 7-iron, while the pitching wedge stays at 43 degrees. Unlike the 2019 model, Titleist published offset figures for this generation, running .122 inches at the 4-iron down to .051 inches at the 48 degree wedge.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a 2023 Titleist T200 7-iron?

The 2023 Titleist T200 7-iron is 30.5 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

Is the 2023 T200 stronger or weaker than the 2019 T200?

Slightly weaker in the middle of the set. The 7-iron moves from 30 to 30.5 degrees and the 8-iron from 34 to 34.5, while the 4-iron and pitching wedge are unchanged.

What is the offset on the 2023 Titleist T200?

Offset runs from .122 inches at the 4-iron down to .051 inches at the 48 degree wedge, with .091 inches at the 7-iron.

Finding a used set

The Titleist T200 (2023) is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
