About the Titleist 712 AP2

The 712 AP2 is the third-generation AP2 and the last of the line to use the traditional 35 degree 7-iron. It is forged from 1025 carbon steel with high-density tungsten placed in the heel and toe to move the centre of gravity low and to the perimeter without enlarging the head. Lofts and offsets carry over unchanged from the 710 AP2; the 714 AP2 that followed strengthened everything from the 6-iron down by a degree. The stock set ran 3-iron through a 51 degree gap wedge, all at D2.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist 712 AP2 7-iron?

The Titleist 712 AP2 7-iron is 35 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the difference between the 712 AP2 and the 714 AP2?

The 714 AP2 is one degree stronger from the 6-iron down. Its 7-iron is 34 degrees against 35 on the 712, and its pitching wedge is 46 degrees against 47.

Do the 710 AP2 and 712 AP2 share the same lofts?

Yes. The 710 AP2 and 712 AP2 carry identical lofts, lies and lengths across the set; the changes between them were in construction and finish.

Finding a used set

The Titleist 712 AP2 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
