About the Titleist T350

The T350 is the 2023 game-improvement iron that replaced the T300, keeping its predecessor's loft ladder exactly — a 29 degree 7-iron and a 43 degree pitching wedge — while shrinking the head slightly and moving to a D1 build rather than D2. It retains the hollow cast body with Max Impact internal support and high-density tungsten weighting. It is the most heavily offset iron in the T-Series, running .213 inches at the 4-iron and .146 inches at the 7-iron, roughly 60 percent more than the T200.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist T350 7-iron?

The Titleist T350 7-iron is 29 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

Is the Titleist T350 the same loft as the T300?

Yes. The T350 carries the T300 loft ladder unchanged, from 20 degrees at the 4-iron to 53 degrees at the second wedge, including a 29 degree 7-iron.

How much offset does the Titleist T350 have?

The T350 is the most offset iron in the T-Series, at .213 inches in the 4-iron and .146 inches in the 7-iron.

Finding a used set

The Titleist T350 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
