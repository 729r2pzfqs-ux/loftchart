About the Titleist 716 AP2

The 716 AP2 is the fifth-generation AP2 and the first to use co-forged construction, with extreme high-density tungsten set low and along the perimeter to raise moment of inertia without growing the head. Lofts carry over unchanged from the 714 AP2, giving a 34 degree 7-iron and a 46 degree pitching wedge, and the offset ladder is trimmed slightly to .110 inches at the 7-iron. The sole is a touch wider than the 714 with trailing-edge relief for cleaner turf interaction. Stock shaft was the ascending-mass True Temper Dynamic Gold AMT.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist 716 AP2 7-iron?

The Titleist 716 AP2 7-iron is 34 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

Do the 714 AP2 and 716 AP2 have the same lofts?

Yes. Both carry a 34 degree 7-iron and a 46 degree pitching wedge across an identical 3-iron to gap wedge ladder; the 716 changed construction rather than loft.

What shaft came stock in the Titleist 716 AP2?

The stock steel shaft was the True Temper Dynamic Gold AMT in R300 and S300, an ascending-mass shaft running 112g to 130g through the set, with Project X LZ as a no-charge alternative.

Finding a used set

The Titleist 716 AP2 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
