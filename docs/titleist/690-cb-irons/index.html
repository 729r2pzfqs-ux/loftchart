About the Titleist 690.CB

The 690.CB is the forged cavity back of Titleist's 2002 tour range and the direct counterpart to the 690.MB muscleback. It keeps traditional players lofts with a 35 degree 7-iron and a 47 degree pitching wedge, and pairs them with the flattest lies in the modern Titleist catalogue: 61.5 degrees at the 7-iron, a full degree and a half flatter than the AP2 sets that followed. Offset is minimal at .110 inches in the 7-iron, and the whole set is built to D2. It was the shorter-shafted, more compact platform that the 695.CB later replaced.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist 690.CB 7-iron?

The Titleist 690.CB 7-iron is 35 degrees, with a 61.5 degree lie and a 36.75 inch standard steel length.

What is the difference between the Titleist 690.CB and 690.MB?

The 690.CB is a forged cavity back and the 690.MB is a muscleback blade. The CB is one degree stronger through the set, with a 35 degree 7-iron against 36, and carries slightly more offset.

How do the 690.CB lies compare with later Titleist irons?

The 690.CB is notably flat: its 7-iron lie is 61.5 degrees, where the 695.CB and the AP2 sets that followed are 63 degrees.

Finding a used set

The Titleist 690.CB is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
