About the Titleist 714 CB

The 714 CB is the compact forged cavity back in Titleist's 2014 range, sitting between the AP2 and the MB. It is forged from 1025 carbon steel with constant blade lengths, a thin topline and minimal progressive offset, from .150 inches at the 2-iron down to .075 inches at the pitching wedge. Lofts are traditional where the AP2 of the same generation had moved stronger: the 7-iron is 35 degrees against 34, and the pitching wedge is 47 degrees against 46. The stock set ran 2-iron through pitching wedge with no gap wedge, and the 716 CB that replaced it carried exactly the same loft ladder.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist 714 CB 7-iron?

The Titleist 714 CB 7-iron is 35 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the difference between the Titleist 714 CB and the 714 AP2?

The CB is the more compact forged cavity back with less offset, and it is one degree weaker through the set: a 35 degree 7-iron and a 47 degree pitching wedge against 34 and 46 on the AP2.

Are the Titleist 714 CB and 716 CB lofts the same?

Yes. Both run an 18 degree 2-iron through a 47 degree pitching wedge with a 35 degree 7-iron, and neither set included a gap wedge.

Finding a used set

The Titleist 714 CB is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
