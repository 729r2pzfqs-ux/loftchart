About the Titleist 695.MB

The 695.MB is the 2006 forged muscleback that replaced the 690.MB and shares its loft ladder exactly: 19 degrees at the 2-iron through 48 degrees at the pitching wedge, with a 36 degree 7-iron. What changed is the geometry around those lofts. Lies are upright by a degree and a half at the 7-iron, 63 degrees against 61.5, and every shaft is three quarters of an inch longer, so the set plays taller and longer than the 690.MB despite identical lofts. It is the direct blade counterpart to the 695.CB.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist 695.MB 7-iron?

The Titleist 695.MB 7-iron is 36 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the difference between the Titleist 695.MB and 690.MB?

The lofts are identical across the set. The 695.MB is a degree and a half more upright at the 7-iron and every club is three quarters of an inch longer.

What is the difference between the 695.MB and the 695.CB?

The 695.MB is a muscleback blade and the 695.CB is a cavity back. The MB is two degrees weaker through the set, with a 36 degree 7-iron against 34 degrees.

Finding a used set

The Titleist 695.MB is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
