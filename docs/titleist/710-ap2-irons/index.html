About the Titleist 710 AP2

The 710 AP2 was the second generation of Titleist's multi-material AP2 players iron, released for the 2010 season. A forged 1025 carbon steel body carries a laser-welded tungsten-nickel weight box in the heel and toe, an elastomer cushion behind the face and an aluminium cavity plate, which together push mass low and to the perimeter while keeping a compact head. Compared with the AP1 of the same generation it has a thinner topline, smaller blade, less offset and a reduced-bounce sole. Lofts are traditional, with a 35 degree 7-iron.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist 710 AP2 7-iron?

The Titleist 710 AP2 7-iron has a loft of 35 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

How do the 710 AP2 lofts compare with the 714 AP2?

From the 6-iron down the 714 AP2 is one degree stronger: the 710 AP2 7-iron is 35 degrees versus 34 degrees in the 714 AP2, and the pitching wedge is 47 degrees versus 46 degrees.

What are the Titleist 710 AP2 irons made of?

The body is forged 1025 carbon steel, with a laser-welded high-density tungsten-nickel weight box, an elastomer cushion behind the face and an aluminium cavity plate.

Finding a used set

The Titleist 710 AP2 ran from 2009 to 2011 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
