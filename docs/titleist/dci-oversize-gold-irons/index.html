About the Titleist DCI Oversize Gold

The DCI Oversize Gold is the 1996 oversize member of the DCI family, the gold-finished counterpart to the DCI Oversize Black and the direct ancestor of the 2001 DCI 822 OS. It is an investment cast oversize cavity back with the heaviest offset ladder Titleist published before the 822 OS: .386 inches at the 1-iron, .180 inches at the 7-iron and .100 inches at the pitching wedge. Lofts run about a degree and a half stronger through the middle of the set than the standard DCI of the era, with a 34.5 degree 7-iron, and the lie angles are two degrees more upright than the DCI Gold at 63 degrees for the 7-iron. Titleist catalogued it from a 1-iron through a lob wedge.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist DCI Oversize Gold 7-iron?

The Titleist DCI Oversize Gold 7-iron is 34.5 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 36.75 inch standard steel length.

How does the DCI Oversize Gold compare with the DCI 822 OS that replaced it?

The 822 OS is a degree stronger through most of the set, with a 33.5 degree 7-iron against 34.5, and it carries even more offset at .205 inches at the 7-iron against .180.

How much offset do the DCI Oversize Gold irons have?

Among the most Titleist has published: .386 inches at the 1-iron, tapering to .180 inches at the 7-iron and .100 inches at the pitching wedge.

Finding a used set

The Titleist DCI Oversize Gold ran from 1996 to 2001 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
