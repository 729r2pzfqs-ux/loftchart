About the Titleist T100

The T100 launched in August 2019 as the tour model of Titleist's new T-Series, replacing the AP2 line. It is a fully forged, multi-material iron in which high-density tungsten is co-forged into the heel and toe of the long and mid irons to control centre-of-gravity progression through the set. The sole uses a Variable Bounce design with a softened trailing edge for turf interaction. Lofts match the outgoing 714/716/718 AP2 family, with a 34 degree 7-iron and a 46 degree pitching wedge.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist T100 7-iron?

The 2019 Titleist T100 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Titleist T100?

The T100 pitching wedge is 46 degrees, and the matching gap wedge, badged W, is 50 degrees.

Are the Titleist T100 lofts the same as the AP2 they replaced?

Yes. The 2019 T100 uses the same lofts as the 714, 716 and 718 AP2, from a 21 degree 3-iron to a 46 degree pitching wedge, though the T100 lie angles are one degree upright in the 3 and 4 irons.

Finding a used set

The Titleist T100 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
