About the Titleist DCI Gold

The DCI Gold is the gold-finished version of the early 1990s Dynamic Cavity Iron that established the DCI name, sold alongside the DCI Black. It is an investment cast perimeter-weighted cavity back with a thick topline and a large rear cavity, aimed at the everyday golfer. Two things date it: the lie angles, which are flat by any modern standard at 61.5 degrees for the 7-iron where later DCI sets use 63, and the offset, which starts at .380 inches at the 1-iron and is still .180 inches at the 7-iron. Lofts are traditional, with a 36 degree 7-iron, and Titleist catalogued a full 1-iron through lob wedge. Every club is built to D1 swing weight.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist DCI Gold 7-iron?

The Titleist DCI Gold 7-iron is 36 degrees, with a 61.5 degree lie and a 36.5 inch standard steel length.

Why do Titleist DCI Gold irons look so flat compared with modern sets?

Because they are. Titleist built the DCI Gold from a 56 degree lie at the 1-iron to 63 degrees at the wedges, roughly two degrees flatter through the set than the DCI 981 and 990 that followed.

How much offset do the Titleist DCI Gold irons have?

A great deal by current standards: .380 inches at the 1-iron, .180 inches at the 7-iron and .095 inches at the pitching wedge.

Finding a used set

The Titleist DCI Gold is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
