About the Titleist 716 CB

The 716 CB is the compact forged cavity back that sits between the AP2 and the MB in Titleist's 2016 range. It is a smaller head with a thinner topline and less offset than the AP2, and it keeps traditional players lofts where the AP2 had moved stronger: the 7-iron is 35 degrees against 34, and the pitching wedge is 47 degrees against 46. High-density tungsten in the toe of the long irons lifts moment of inertia without widening the blade. The stock set ran 2-iron through pitching wedge with no gap wedge.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist 716 CB 7-iron?

The Titleist 716 CB 7-iron is 35 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the difference between the Titleist 716 CB and 716 AP2?

The CB is the more compact head with less offset, .102 inches at the 7-iron against .110 on the AP2, and it is one degree weaker through the middle of the set.

Does the Titleist 716 CB come with a gap wedge?

No. The 716 CB stock set ran 2-iron through a 47 degree pitching wedge; a gap wedge was not part of the set.

Finding a used set

The Titleist 716 CB is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
