About the Titleist 681 Forged

The 681 Forged is the 2001 blade that reopened Titleist's forged players line and led directly to the 690 family. It is forged from soft 1030 mild carbon steel with a thin sole of moderate camber and a constant .080 inches of offset from the 2-iron down, which is the least progressive offset ladder in the Titleist archive. Lofts are traditional, with a 36 degree 7-iron and a 49 degree pitching wedge, and lies are notably flat: 62 degrees at the 7-iron against 63 on most later Titleist sets. The 1-iron was catalogued but Titleist published no offset or bounce figure for it, so neither is recorded here. A tour-only 681 "T" variant was offered alongside it.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist 681 Forged 7-iron?

The Titleist 681 Forged 7-iron is 36 degrees, with a 62 degree lie and a 36.75 inch standard steel length.

How much offset does the Titleist 681 Forged have?

A constant .080 inches from the 2-iron through the pitching wedge. Titleist did not publish an offset figure for the 1-iron.

How do the 681 Forged lofts compare with the 690.MB that replaced it?

The lofts match exactly from the 2-iron through the 9-iron, including a 36 degree 7-iron. The only loft change is the pitching wedge, 48 degrees on the 690.MB against 49 on the 681, and the 690.MB lies are up to half a degree flatter.

Finding a used set

The Titleist 681 Forged ran from 2001 to 2002 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
