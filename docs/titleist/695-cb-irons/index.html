About the Titleist 695.CB

The Forged 695.CB was Titleist's shallow-cavity players iron, launched alongside the 695.MB muscleback and the 775.CB. Both 695 models were forged from 1025 mild carbon steel and used a Z-Cavity design that removed mass from the heel to move the centre of gravity closer to the centre of the face. Lofts are traditional, with a 34 degree 7-iron and a 46 degree pitching wedge. Note that although the model is often associated with the 2004-2005 era because Titleist staff players used prototypes on tour from mid-2005, the retail launch was April 2006.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Titleist 695.CB 7-iron?

The Titleist 695.CB 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, with a 63 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

When did the Titleist 695.CB irons come out?

Titleist announced the Forged 695.CB in April 2006, alongside the 695.MB and 775.CB, though tour staff had played prototypes since mid-2005.

What are the Titleist 695.CB irons made of?

They are forged from 1025 mild carbon steel and use a Z-Cavity design that shifts mass away from the heel.

Finding a used set

The Titleist 695.CB is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
