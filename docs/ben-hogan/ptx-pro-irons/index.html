About the Ben Hogan PTx Pro

The PTx Pro replaced the PTx in April 2019 and keeps its predecessor's three-piece, multi-step, multi-material forging while moving to a slightly larger face profile and wider sole. The long irons pair a soft 1025 carbon steel body with an ultra-thin forged MS300 face and co-forged tungsten weight out at the toe; the scoring irons swap that tungsten for lightweight titanium co-forged between body and face to keep the flight low and penetrating. Like the Ft. Worth Black and ICON it abandons the loft-stamped PreciseLoft heads of the original PTx and is sold by conventional club number, though the 4-degree loft gapping is unchanged: 4-iron at 22 degrees through pitching wedge at 46 degrees. Stock sets were 4-PW, 5-PW or 6-PW at 770, 690 and 600 USD, in nickel chrome or the Diamond Black Metal finish. It is still in the Ben Hogan range alongside the later PTx Tour, which is a separate model on stronger 21.5-to-45.5-degree lofts.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ben Hogan PTx Pro 7-iron?

The Ben Hogan PTx Pro 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, a lie of 62.5 degrees, a length of 37.00 inches and 0.130 inches of offset.

Are Ben Hogan PTx Pro irons stamped with the loft, like the original PTx?

No. The original PTx carried its exact loft on the sole under Ben Hogan's PreciseLoft system, with the iron number only on the hosel. The PTx Pro is sold by conventional club number, 4-iron through pitching wedge, while keeping the same 4-degree loft gaps from 22 to 46 degrees.

What clubs come in a stock Ben Hogan PTx Pro set?

Ben Hogan offered it as a 4-PW seven-piece set at 770 USD, a 5-PW six-piece set at 690 USD or a 6-PW five-piece set at 600 USD, in either a nickel chrome or a Diamond Black Metal finish.

How does the PTx Pro differ from the PTx it replaced?

It is slightly larger in face profile and sole width, keeps the co-forged multi-material build but revises where the tungsten and titanium sit, uses an updated V-Sole, and is numbered conventionally rather than by loft.

Finding a used set

The Ben Hogan PTx Pro is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
