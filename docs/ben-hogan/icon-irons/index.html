About the Ben Hogan ICON

The ICON is Ben Hogan Golf's traditional forged muscle-back blade, announced in March 2020 as the better-player iron in the range alongside the PTx Pro. It is individually forged from 1025 carbon steel with a thin topline, compact head and minimal offset, running from 0.120 inches at the 4-iron to 0.068 inches at the pitching wedge, and uses the enhanced V-Sole with a high-bounce leading edge and a lower-bounce trailing edge. Unlike the Ft. Worth 15 and PTx that preceded it, the ICON is sold by conventional club number, with the iron number etched in red along the sole, though it keeps Ben Hogan's 4-degree loft gapping throughout. The stock set is seven clubs, 4-iron at 22 degrees through pitching wedge at 46 degrees, offered in nickel chrome or the darker Diamond Black Metal finish. Launch pricing was $770 for the chrome set and $800 for the black.

Frequently asked questions

Are Ben Hogan ICON irons stamped with the loft or with a club number?

With a club number. The ICON has the iron number etched in red along the sole, unlike the earlier Ft. Worth 15 and PTx irons, which the relaunched Ben Hogan company stamped with the loft in degrees instead. The ICON still uses Ben Hogan's consistent 4-degree loft gaps, so the numbers map cleanly onto lofts: 4-iron 22 degrees through pitching wedge 46 degrees.

What is the loft of a Ben Hogan ICON 7-iron?

The Ben Hogan ICON 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, a lie of 62.5 degrees and a length of 37.00 inches, with 0.092 inches of offset.

What clubs come in a stock Ben Hogan ICON set?

Seven clubs, 4-iron through pitching wedge. It was offered in a nickel chrome finish at $770 and a Diamond Black Metal finish at $800.

Where it sits in the lineup

Introduced in 2020, the Ben Hogan ICON is a current blade set in the Ben Hogan range, with no replacement announced. It replaced the Ben Hogan Ft. Worth Black. New sets are built to the standard lofts, lies and lengths in the chart above, though a fitting can move any of them — so confirm the numbers on the order sheet. Used sets appear on eBay, 2nd Swing and Golf Avenue, and should be measured against this chart before buying.
