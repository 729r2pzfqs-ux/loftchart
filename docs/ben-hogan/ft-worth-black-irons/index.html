About the Ben Hogan Ft. Worth Black

The Ft. Worth Black is the second-generation Ft. Worth blade, introduced in May 2018 on the same single-piece forged chassis as the Ft. Worth 15 but with reshaped Depression Cavities, a revised Adaptive Perimeter Weighting System and a V-Sole retuned iron by iron - a stronger leading edge on some heads and a softer trailing edge on others. It is the model where Ben Hogan retreated from its loft-stamping convention: rather than sell heads marked 20 to 47 degrees in four launch profiles, the company kept only the mid-high profile and stamped traditional club numbers on the sole, so the set is a plain 4-iron at 22 degrees through pitching wedge at 46 degrees in 4-degree gaps. Black refers to the proprietary Diamond Black Metal finish; the identical head in nickel chrome was sold as the Ft. Worth White. The stock set was seven clubs, 4-PW, at 735 USD, and the line was replaced by the ICON blade in 2020.

Frequently asked questions

Are Ben Hogan Ft. Worth Black irons stamped with the loft or with a club number?

With a club number. The Ft. Worth Black is the model where Ben Hogan stopped selling the Ft. Worth line by loft number. The company kept its 4-degree PreciseLoft gapping but dropped the four launch profiles down to the mid-high profile alone, so the heads carry traditional numbers 4 through PW covering 22 to 46 degrees.

What is the loft of a Ben Hogan Ft. Worth Black 7-iron?

The Ben Hogan Ft. Worth Black 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, a lie of 62.5 degrees, a length of 37.00 inches and 0.092 inches of offset.

What is the difference between the Ft. Worth Black and the Ft. Worth White?

Finish only. Both are the same forged head; the Black wears Ben Hogan's Diamond Black Metal finish, which reduces glare and gives better contrast against the ball, and the White wears a traditional nickel chrome.

What clubs come in a stock Ben Hogan Ft. Worth Black set?

Seven clubs, 4-iron through pitching wedge, at 735 USD. Unlike the Ft. Worth 15 before it, there is only one loft matrix to choose from.

Finding a used set

The Ben Hogan Ft. Worth Black is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
