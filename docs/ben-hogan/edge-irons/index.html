About the Ben Hogan Edge

The Edge is the most forgiving iron in the relaunched Ben Hogan line and revives a name the original company used in the late 1980s. The head is built from two forged parts - a hydraulically hammered face laser welded to a separately forged back frame and hosel - producing a hollow body that is then filled with polycarbonate foam, with a progressively thicker face through the scoring irons. Ben Hogan states the heads are on average 12 percent larger than its PTx irons and carry noticeably more offset, from 0.210 inches at the 4-iron down to 0.120 inches at the pitching wedge. It is the model where Ben Hogan abandoned the loft-stamping convention: the company kept the 4-degree PreciseLoft gapping but dropped the four launch profiles down to the single mid-high profile and stamped traditional club numbers, so the set runs 4-iron at 22 degrees through pitching wedge at 46 degrees. Stock sets were sold as 4-PW, 5-PW or 6-PW. It was later replaced in the line-up by the Edge EX, which moved to stronger 20-to-44-degree lofts.

Frequently asked questions

Are Ben Hogan Edge irons stamped with the loft or with a club number?

With a club number. The Edge is the model where Ben Hogan stepped back from the loft-stamping convention it used on the Ft. Worth 15 and PTx. The company kept the 4-degree PreciseLoft gapping but reduced the four launch profiles to the single mid-high profile, so the set is simply a 4-iron through pitching wedge running from 22 to 46 degrees.

What is the loft of a Ben Hogan Edge 7-iron?

The Ben Hogan Edge 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, a lie of 62.5 degrees and a length of 37.00 inches. That is markedly weaker than most game-improvement 7-irons of the same era, which were commonly 30 degrees or stronger.

What clubs come in a stock Ben Hogan Edge set?

Ben Hogan sold the Edge as a 4-PW seven-piece set, a 5-PW six-piece set or a 6-PW five-piece set, priced at $735, $660 and $575 respectively.

Finding a used set

The Ben Hogan Edge is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
