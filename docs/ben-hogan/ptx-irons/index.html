About the Ben Hogan PTx

The PTx was Ben Hogan Golf's second iron after the Ft. Worth 15 and was introduced in January 2016 as a more forgiving, technology-led companion to the blade. Ben Hogan describes it as a transitional iron: the long irons are hollow, with a 1025 carbon steel frame and an ultra-thin MS300 steel face plus low tungsten weighting, while the scoring irons are co-forged with a lightweight titanium core in place of steel. Like the Ft. Worth 15 it uses the PreciseLoft system of 4-degree loft gaps, and each head carries its exact loft stamped on the sole, with the traditional iron number on the hosel. A stock set is seven clubs in one of four launch profiles - low 20/24/28/32/36/40/44, mid 21/25/29/33/37/41/45, mid-high 22/26/30/34/38/42/46 or high 23/27/31/35/39/43/47 - and the rows below are the low-launch profile, since length and lie are set by the loft band rather than by the profile. It was succeeded by the PTx Pro.

Frequently asked questions

Why do Ben Hogan PTx irons say 32 instead of 7-iron?

Ben Hogan's PreciseLoft system stamps the actual loft in degrees on the sole of each PTx head rather than selling the set by club number. The traditional iron number is engraved on the hosel as a secondary marking. A set is seven heads in 4-degree gaps covering 20 to 47 degrees depending on which launch profile you order.

What is the loft of the middle iron in a Ben Hogan PTx set?

The fourth club of the seven-piece set is 32 degrees in the low-launch profile, with a 62.5-degree lie and a 37.00-inch steel length. That club is the 7-iron equivalent, and it is 33, 34 or 35 degrees in the mid, mid-high and high profiles respectively.

Is the Ben Hogan PTx a players iron or a game-improvement iron?

Ben Hogan positioned it as a transitional iron between the two - a forged, multi-material head with tungsten weighting and hollow long irons, a slightly larger profile and a little more offset than the Ft. Worth 15 blade, but far more compact and weaker-lofted than a true game-improvement iron.

Finding a used set

The Ben Hogan PTx is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
