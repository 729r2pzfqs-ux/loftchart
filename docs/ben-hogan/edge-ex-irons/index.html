About the Ben Hogan Edge EX

The Edge EX is Ben Hogan's game-improvement iron, announced on 13 April 2021 and shipping from late April that year. It replaces the hollow, foam-filled body of the Edge with an Open Cavity Design in which weight is pulled out of the area behind the face and pushed to the toe, heel and sole, leaving the forged face visually floating between the perimeter rails; the head is larger than the Edge's and carries a great deal of offset, from 0.230 inches at the 4-iron to 0.160 inches at the pitching wedge. Like the Edge it is numbered conventionally rather than by loft, and it keeps Ben Hogan's 4-degree gapping, but every loft is 2 degrees stronger than the Edge, ICON and PTx Pro: 4-iron at 20 degrees through pitching wedge at 44 degrees, giving a 32-degree 7-iron. Sets were sold as 4-PW, 5-PW or 6-PW at 800, 720 and 630 USD.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ben Hogan Edge EX 7-iron?

The Ben Hogan Edge EX 7-iron has a loft of 32 degrees, a lie of 62.5 degrees, a length of 37.00 inches and 0.195 inches of offset.

How do Edge EX lofts compare with the Ben Hogan Edge?

Every Edge EX club is 2 degrees stronger. The Edge runs 22 to 46 degrees from 4-iron to pitching wedge; the Edge EX runs 20 to 44. Both keep Ben Hogan's consistent 4-degree gaps, so the shape of the set is unchanged.

Are Edge EX irons stamped with the loft rather than a club number?

No. Ben Hogan sold its Ft. Worth 15 and PTx irons with the exact loft stamped on the sole, but from the Edge onwards the company reverted to conventional club numbers, and the Edge EX is numbered 4 through PW.

What clubs come in a stock Ben Hogan Edge EX set?

Ben Hogan offered a 4-PW seven-piece set at 800 USD, a 5-PW six-piece set at 720 USD and a 6-PW five-piece set at 630 USD, all custom built to order.

Finding a used set

The Ben Hogan Edge EX is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
