About the Cobra AMP

The AMP is Cobra's 2012 game-improvement iron, the set that carried the AMP name a year before the metalwood-face-welded AMP Cell replaced it. Cobra branded the weighting scheme Advanced Material Placement: a multi-alloy body that pushes mass around to give livelier long irons and steadier short irons, with a full-cavity thermoplastic urethane layer for damping. The face of the 3- through 7-irons is hyper 17-4 stainless steel milled thinner to Cobra's E9 variable-thickness pattern, and a V-Skid sole narrows at the heel and toe. Lofts and lengths carry straight over from the S3: the 7-iron is 31 degrees on 37.25 inches and the pitching wedge 44 degrees. The stock set is eight clubs, 4-iron through pitching wedge plus a gap wedge, in steel or graphite, with a 3-iron, sand wedge and lob wedge catalogued separately. Swing weights below are the steel-shaft values — graphite builds run one point lighter. Cobra did not publish an offset figure for this set.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cobra AMP 7-iron?

The Cobra AMP 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, a lie of 62.5 degrees and a length of 37.25 inches.

What clubs come in a stock Cobra AMP set?

The stock set is eight clubs — 4-iron through pitching wedge plus a gap wedge — in steel or graphite. Cobra also catalogued a 3-iron, sand wedge and lob wedge.

What is the difference between the Cobra AMP and the Cobra AMP Cell irons?

The AMP is a conventional cast multi-alloy cavity back with an E9 face. The AMP Cell that replaced it in 2013 welds a thin high-strength steel face onto a softer 431 stainless body in the 4- through 7-irons. Their 7-iron through pitching wedge lofts are identical; the AMP Cell is a degree stronger from the 3- through the 6-iron.

Finding a used set

The Cobra AMP is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
