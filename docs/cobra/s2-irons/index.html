About the Cobra S2

The S2 is Cobra's multi-material game-improvement iron, announced on 26 August 2009 and shipping from 1 September 2009 as the 2010 line — the direct ancestor of the E9 cavity that Cobra used through the S3 and AMP sets. A 431 stainless steel body is lightened with a polymer topline and toe insert, an expanded urethane sole insert and an aluminium back plate, which moves discretionary weight low into the heel and toe, and Cobra called the face pattern 9 Point Face Technology. A mid-width stepped sole handles turf interaction. Lofts are strong for the period at 31 degrees in the 7-iron and 43 degrees in the pitching wedge, on a 37.25-inch 7-iron. The stock men's set is 4-iron through pitching wedge plus a gap wedge, in steel or graphite; a Lite/Seniors set ran 5-iron through sand wedge, and Cobra also catalogued a 3-iron and a lob wedge. Cobra published a single length column covering both graphite and steel builds, and did not publish an offset figure for this set.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cobra S2 7-iron?

The Cobra S2 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, a lie of 63 degrees and a length of 37.25 inches.

What clubs come in a stock Cobra S2 set?

The stock men's set is a 4-iron through pitching wedge plus a gap wedge, in steel or graphite. A Lite/Seniors set ran 5-iron through sand wedge, and Cobra also catalogued a 3-iron and a lob wedge.

What year did the Cobra S2 irons come out?

Cobra announced the S2 irons on 26 August 2009 and began shipping them on 1 September 2009. They were sold as the 2010 line and were replaced by the Cobra S3 in February 2011.

Finding a used set

The Cobra S2 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
