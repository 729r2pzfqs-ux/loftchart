About the Cobra Aerojet

The Aerojet is Cobra's 2023 game-improvement iron and the standard-length version of a set also sold in ONE Length. It combines a PWRSHELL face insert with H.O.T. Face Technology, a variable-thickness pattern generated by machine learning to even out ball speed and spin across the face, and a PWR-BRIDGE weight suspended in a soft polymer filler that positions mass low while damping impact sound. Lofts are identical to the LTDx set it replaced, with a 26.5-degree 7-iron and a 41.5-degree pitching wedge on a 37.25-inch 7-iron. The stock set is sold as 4-iron through pitching wedge or 5-iron through gap wedge, in right and left hand and in both steel and graphite. Swing weights below are the steel-shaft values, and offsets are converted from Cobra's published millimetre figures.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cobra Aerojet 7-iron?

The Cobra Aerojet 7-iron has a loft of 26.5 degrees, a lie of 63.5 degrees and a length of 37.25 inches.

What clubs come in a stock Cobra Aerojet set?

Cobra sold the Aerojet irons as a 4-iron through pitching wedge set or a 5-iron through gap wedge set, both seven clubs. A sand wedge was also catalogued.

Are these the Aerojet or the Aerojet ONE Length specs?

These are the standard-length Aerojet irons. The Aerojet ONE Length version builds every iron to 7-iron length and has its own specifications.

Finding a used set

The Cobra Aerojet is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
