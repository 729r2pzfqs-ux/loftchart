About the Cobra Fly-Z

The Fly-Z is Cobra's 2015 game-improvement iron and the standard-length model in a four-club family that also included the Fly-Z XL, the Fly-Z Pro and the forged Fly-Z+. It pairs a 17-4 stainless steel body with a high-strength 455 stainless face insert over Cobra's deepest undercut cavity to that point, adding a Speed Channel around the perimeter of the face and a thermoplastic urethane Harmonic Cavity insert behind the impact zone for damping. Lofts are moderately strong, with a 30.5-degree 7-iron and a 45-degree pitching wedge, on a 37.25-inch steel 7-iron. The stock set is seven clubs, 4-iron through gap wedge, in both steel and graphite; 3-iron, sand wedge and lob wedge heads were also catalogued. Lengths and swing weights below are the steel-shaft values, and offsets are converted from Cobra's published millimetre figures.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cobra Fly-Z 7-iron?

The Cobra Fly-Z 7-iron has a loft of 30.5 degrees, a lie of 62.5 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 37.25 inches.

What clubs come in a stock Cobra Fly-Z set?

The stock set is seven clubs, 4-iron through gap wedge, in either steel or graphite. A 3-iron, sand wedge and lob wedge were available separately.

What is the difference between the Fly-Z and the Fly-Z XL or Fly-Z Pro?

The Fly-Z is the standard game-improvement model. The Fly-Z XL is a larger, lighter and more forgiving set, the Fly-Z Pro is a compact better-player iron, and the Fly-Z+ is a forged model.

Finding a used set

The Cobra Fly-Z is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
