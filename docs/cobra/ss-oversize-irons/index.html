About the Cobra King Cobra SS Oversize

The King Cobra SS Oversize is Cobra's 2002 oversize game-improvement iron and the set the SS-i Oversize replaced a year later. It is investment cast from 17-4 stainless steel, with tungsten inserts in the 2-, 3- and 4-irons to drop the centre of gravity in the hardest clubs to launch, and a skid sole for turf interaction. Lofts are traditional: the 7-iron is 34 degrees and the pitching wedge 45 degrees, roughly a degree weaker through the middle of the set than the SS-i that followed. Cobra sold the stock set as 3-iron through pitching wedge, with a 2-iron and gap, sand and lob wedges available separately, in both right and left hand. Cobra's own published table for this set gives lengths for graphite builds only; the steel lengths below are the graphite figures less half an inch, which is exactly the graphite-to-steel offset Cobra published for the successor SS-i Oversize across an identical length ladder — see `sources`. Cobra also published an offset column whose values run backwards relative to normal convention (increasing toward the wedges), so, as with the SS-i record, they are not reproduced here.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a King Cobra SS Oversize 7-iron?

The King Cobra SS Oversize 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, a lie of 62.5 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 37.00 inches.

What clubs come in a stock King Cobra SS Oversize set?

The stock set is a 3-iron through pitching wedge. Cobra also sold a 2-iron and gap, sand and lob wedges as separate heads, in both right and left hand.

How do the King Cobra SS Oversize lofts compare with the SS-i Oversize?

The SS Oversize is about a degree weaker through the middle of the set — 34 degrees at the 7-iron against 33 degrees for the SS-i — while both share a 45-degree pitching wedge.

Finding a used set

The Cobra King Cobra SS Oversize is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
