About the Cobra DS-ADAPT

The DS-ADAPT is Cobra's 2025 game-improvement iron, the direct replacement for the Darkspeed and the standard model in a family that also includes the super-game-improvement DS-ADAPT MAX. Every iron keeps the metalwood-inspired hollow body, but the face insert is now the 360 SPEEDSHELL, wrapping further around the toe and sole; paired with H.O.T. Face variable thickness, Cobra claims 23 per cent more face flex than the Darkspeed. A re-engineered internal PWR-BRIDGE weight sits lower for a lower centre of gravity, and a softer foam fills the cavity for damping. Lofts and lengths carry over from the Darkspeed almost unchanged — the 7-iron is 27 degrees on 37.25 inches and the pitching wedge 42 degrees — with the 4-iron half a degree weaker and the gap wedge half a degree stronger. Stock steel sets are sold as 5-iron through gap wedge or 4-iron through pitching wedge; graphite is a 5-GW set, and a 5H plus 6-GW combo set was also offered. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cobra DS-ADAPT 7-iron?

The Cobra DS-ADAPT 7-iron has a loft of 27 degrees, a lie of 63.5 degrees and a length of 37.25 inches.

What clubs come in a stock Cobra DS-ADAPT set?

Stock steel sets are 5-iron through gap wedge or 4-iron through pitching wedge. Graphite is sold as a 5-GW set, and Cobra also offered a combo set with a 5-hybrid and 6-iron through gap wedge.

How do the Cobra DS-ADAPT irons differ from the Darkspeed?

The lofts are nearly identical. The change is the face: DS-ADAPT uses the larger 360 SPEEDSHELL insert that wraps around the toe and sole, which Cobra says flexes 23 per cent more than the Darkspeed face, together with a lower PWR-BRIDGE internal weight and softer cavity foam.

What is the difference between the DS-ADAPT and DS-ADAPT MAX irons?

The MAX is the super-game-improvement version, with more offset and weaker lofts — a 29-degree 7-iron and a 43.5-degree pitching wedge against 27 and 42 degrees for the standard DS-ADAPT.

Finding a used set

The Cobra DS-ADAPT is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
