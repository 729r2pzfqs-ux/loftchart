About the Cobra KING F9 Speedback

The KING F9 Speedback is Cobra's 2019 game-improvement iron and the standard-length version of a set also sold in ONE Length. Cobra carried the SPEEDBACK name over from the driver: a belt of steel low and wide across the back that drops and deepens the centre of gravity without widening the sole. The face is a forged E9 PWRSHELL insert, 1.8 mm at its thinnest, over a deep undercut sole with a 1.2 mm internal Speed Channel, and 33 grams of tungsten sit in the heel and toe of the 4- through 7-irons. A three-piece medallion of aluminium, thermoplastic polyurethane and acrylic foam handles damping, and hosel lengths step up through the set. Lofts are moderately strong for the period at 29.5 degrees in the 7-iron and 44 degrees in the pitching wedge, on a 37.25-inch steel 7-iron. The stock set is seven clubs, 5-iron through gap wedge, with a 4-iron and sand wedge catalogued separately. Lengths and swing weights below are the standard steel-shaft values; Cobra also published a COBRA CONNECT build a quarter of an inch longer at every club. Offsets are converted from Cobra's published millimetre figures.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cobra KING F9 Speedback 7-iron?

The Cobra KING F9 Speedback 7-iron has a loft of 29.5 degrees, a lie of 63.5 degrees and a standard steel-shaft length of 37.25 inches.

What clubs come in a stock Cobra KING F9 Speedback set?

The stock steel set is seven clubs, 5-iron through gap wedge. A 4-iron and a sand wedge were catalogued separately, and Cobra also sold the set in ONE Length.

Why are there two lengths listed for the Cobra KING F9 Speedback irons?

Cobra published a standard build and a COBRA CONNECT build fitted with sensor-equipped grips. The COBRA CONNECT clubs are a quarter of an inch longer at every position; the specs here are the standard lengths.

Finding a used set

The Cobra KING F9 Speedback ran from 2019 to 2020 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
