About the Cobra S3

The S3 is Cobra's 2011 game-improvement iron, built around E9 Face Technology — a variable-thickness face designed to enlarge the effective hitting zone, with an oval sweet zone in the long irons and a rounder one in the short irons. A hidden internal polymer topline and a full-cavity TPU insert redistribute weight and damp vibration, and a stepped crescent sole narrows at the heel and toe for turf interaction. Long irons (3-7) use 17-4 Hyper Steel while the 8-iron down uses standard 17-4 stainless. Lofts are strong for the era at 31 degrees in the 7-iron and 44 degrees in the pitching wedge, and lengths run long (37.25 inches at the 7-iron). The stock men's set is 4-9, PW and GW; a Seniors/Lite set ran 5-9, PW, GW, SW, and iron-hybrid combo sets were also sold. Some secondary listings date the S3 to 2013; that is wrong — Cobra announced it on 15 February 2011 and the manufacturer product page is titled "2011 Cobra S3 Irons". Swing weights shown are the R/S values; Lite-flex clubs run one point lighter.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cobra S3 7-iron?

The Cobra S3 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, a lie of 62.5 degrees and a length of 37.25 inches.

What year did the Cobra S3 irons come out?

The Cobra S3 irons were announced on 15 February 2011 and sold as Cobra's 2011 game-improvement iron.

What are the Cobra S3 wedge lofts?

The pitching wedge is 44 degrees, the gap wedge 49 degrees, the sand wedge 54 degrees and the lob wedge 59 degrees.

Finding a used set

The Cobra S3 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
