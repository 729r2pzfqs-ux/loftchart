About the Cobra LTDx

The LTDx is Cobra's 2022 game-improvement iron and the standard-length version of a set that was also sold in ONE Length. Its defining feature is a steel core bar that centres the centre of gravity behind the hitting zone, suspended in a soft polymer so the head still feels damped. The PWRSHELL face is an L-cup design forged from thin, strong stainless steel with a thin area about 23 per cent larger than the previous generation, wrapping further onto the sole, and the face and grooves are CNC milled. Lofts are among the strongest Cobra has published, with a 26.5-degree 7-iron and a 41.5-degree pitching wedge on a 37.25-inch 7-iron. The stock set is seven clubs, 5-iron through gap wedge in right and left hand, with a 4-PW option in right hand; graphite was a custom-order option. Swing weights below are the steel-shaft values, and offsets are converted from Cobra's published millimetre figures. Cobra notes that an Arccos sensor grip adds a quarter of an inch to the listed lengths.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cobra LTDx 7-iron?

The Cobra LTDx 7-iron has a loft of 26.5 degrees, a lie of 63.5 degrees and a length of 37.25 inches.

What clubs come in a stock Cobra LTDx set?

The stock set is seven clubs, 5-iron through gap wedge in right and left hand, with a 4-iron through pitching wedge option in right hand. A sand wedge was also catalogued.

Are these the LTDx or the LTDx ONE Length specs?

These are the standard-length LTDx irons. The LTDx ONE Length version builds every iron to 7-iron length and has its own specifications.

Finding a used set

The Cobra LTDx is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
