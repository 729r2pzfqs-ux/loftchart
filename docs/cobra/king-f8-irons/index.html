About the Cobra KING F8

The KING F8 is Cobra's 2018 game-improvement iron and the standard-length version of a set that was also offered in ONE Length. It is a progressive set: the long irons are hollow bodies for a lower centre of gravity and a more responsive face, the mid and scoring irons are cavity backs with a two-piece carbon fibre insert and medallion, and the gap, sand and lob wedges use a specialty wedge design with a carbon fibre insert. All are fronted by a forged PWRSHELL face. It was the first Cobra iron set shipped with COBRA CONNECT grip sensors as standard, which add a quarter of an inch to each club. Lofts are strong, with a 29.5-degree 7-iron and a 44-degree pitching wedge; the stock set is seven clubs, 5-iron through gap wedge. Lengths below are the standard-grip figures rather than the COBRA CONNECT ones, swing weights are the steel-shaft values, and offsets are converted from Cobra's published millimetre figures.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cobra KING F8 7-iron?

The Cobra KING F8 7-iron has a loft of 29.5 degrees, a lie of 62.5 degrees and a standard length of 37.25 inches.

What clubs come in a stock Cobra KING F8 set?

The stock set is seven clubs, 5-iron through gap wedge. A 4-iron, sand wedge and lob wedge were also catalogued.

Why are there two lengths listed for the Cobra KING F8?

The F8 shipped with COBRA CONNECT sensor grips, which are a quarter of an inch longer than a standard grip. The specs here use the standard-grip lengths.

Finding a used set

The Cobra KING F8 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
