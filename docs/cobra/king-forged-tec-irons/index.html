About the Cobra KING Forged Tec

The KING Forged Tec is Cobra's multi-material players-distance iron: a 10-25 carbon steel body forged five times, fitted with a thinner forged J15 high-strength steel face insert, co-forged tungsten weights in the heel and toe, and a TPU insert behind the impact zone for damping. It sits between Cobra's Forged CB/MB blades and its cast game-improvement irons, with a compact but slightly thicker profile and modest offset (3.0 mm at the 7-iron). Lofts are moderately strong, with a 30-degree 7-iron and a 45-degree pitching wedge. The stock set is eight clubs, 4-iron through gap wedge. It reached retail in November 2016 as Cobra's 2017-season forged iron. Offsets below are converted from Cobra's published millimetre figures.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cobra KING Forged Tec 7-iron?

The Cobra KING Forged Tec 7-iron has a loft of 30 degrees, a lie of 62.5 degrees and a length of 37.25 inches.

What clubs come in a stock Cobra KING Forged Tec set?

The stock set is eight clubs, 4-iron through gap wedge. A 3-iron was also offered, and the gap wedge is 50 degrees.

What is the Cobra KING Forged Tec made of?

The body is a five-times-forged 10-25 carbon steel with a forged J15 high-strength steel face insert, co-forged tungsten heel and toe weights, and a TPU damping insert.

Finding a used set

The Cobra KING Forged Tec ran from 2016 to 2018 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
