About the Mizuno JPX-825

The JPX-825 was Mizuno's cast game-improvement iron for 2013 and 2014, sitting below the forged JPX-825 Pro in the same family. It uses a deep pocket cavity and a thin, high-strength face, with a wide sole and a low centre of gravity in the long irons. Lofts are strong for the period, with a 32-degree 7-iron and a 45-degree pitching wedge, and the long irons carry negative measured bounce. The stock set is 4-iron through gap wedge. Lofts, lies and lengths listed are the men's standard steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno JPX-825 7-iron?

The Mizuno JPX-825 7-iron has a loft of 32 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the difference between the JPX-825 and the JPX-825 Pro?

The JPX-825 is the cast, wider-soled game-improvement model. The JPX-825 Pro is a forged, more compact iron with lofts about one degree weaker through the long and mid irons.

What clubs come in a JPX-825 set?

The stock range is 4-iron through gap wedge, with the pitching wedge at 45 degrees and the gap wedge at 50 degrees.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno JPX-825 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
