About the Mizuno JPX-923 Hot Metal

The JPX-923 Hot Metal is the cast, distance-oriented model of the 2022 JPX family, using a Nickel Chromoly 4335 body with a face thinned to 1.8 mm and a redesigned V-Chassis sole. It was sold alongside narrower Hot Metal Pro and weaker-lofted Hot Metal HL variants; the specs here are the standard Hot Metal. It carries the most offset in the family, from 0.233 inches in the 4-iron to 0.065 inches in the lob wedge, and the strongest lofts, with a 28.5-degree 7-iron and a 42.5-degree pitching wedge against 30 and 44 degrees on the JPX-923 Forged. The stock set is 4-iron through lob wedge. Lofts, lies and lengths listed are the men's standard steel-shaft build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno JPX-923 Hot Metal 7-iron?

The Mizuno JPX-923 Hot Metal 7-iron has a loft of 28.5 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

How do JPX-923 Hot Metal lofts compare with JPX-923 Forged?

The Hot Metal is 1.5 degrees stronger through most of the set. Its 7-iron is 28.5 degrees against 30 degrees for the Forged, and its pitching wedge is 42.5 degrees against 44 degrees.

What is the difference between JPX-923 Hot Metal, Hot Metal Pro and Hot Metal HL?

All three share the same head material. Hot Metal Pro is a more compact head with less offset at the same lofts, while Hot Metal HL is weaker-lofted for higher launch, with a 31-degree 7-iron against 28.5 degrees on the standard Hot Metal.

What wedges were in the JPX-923 Hot Metal set?

The stock range was 4-iron through lob wedge, with a 42.5-degree pitching wedge, 48-degree gap wedge, 54-degree sand wedge and 60-degree lob wedge.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno JPX-923 Hot Metal is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
