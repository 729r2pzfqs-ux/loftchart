About the Mizuno Pro 221

The Mizuno Pro 221 is the one-piece muscle-back of the 2022 Mizuno Pro series, replacing the MP-20 MB and sitting alongside the cavity-backed Pro 223 and the hollow-body Pro 225. It is Grain Flow Forged HD from a single billet of 1025E Pure Select mild carbon steel in Hiroshima, with a soft copper underlay beneath the nickel chrome plating. Lofts are traditional for a blade, with a 34-degree 7-iron and a 46-degree pitching wedge, and offset is the smallest of the three 2022 models at 0.087 inch in the 7-iron. The stock set is 3-PW in right hand. Lofts, lies and lengths listed are the men's standard steel-shaft build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno Pro 221 7-iron?

The Mizuno Pro 221 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Mizuno Pro 221?

The Mizuno Pro 221 pitching wedge is 46 degrees. The stock right-handed set runs 3-PW, so there is no gap wedge in the set.

How do Mizuno Pro 221 lofts compare to the Pro 223 and Pro 225?

The Pro 221 is the weakest lofted of the three 2022 models at 34 degrees in the 7-iron, against 32 degrees in the Pro 223 and 30 degrees in the Pro 225.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno Pro 221 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
