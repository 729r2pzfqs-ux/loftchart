About the Mizuno MP-32

The MP-32 is a Grain Flow Forged cavity blade that pairs a blade-like profile and very small offset with a shallow cavity and a Cut Muscle pad behind the hitting area. It sits between the MP-33 blade and Mizuno's larger cavity irons, keeping the same 4-degree loft gapping through the short irons but running one degree stronger than the MP-33 in the mid and short irons. Lengths and lofts listed are the standard men's steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno MP-32 7-iron?

The Mizuno MP-32 7-iron has a loft of 35 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

How much offset do the Mizuno MP-32 irons have?

Very little. Offset runs 0.120 inches in the long irons and drops to 0.110 inches from the 7-iron through the pitching wedge.

Is the MP-32 a blade or a cavity back?

It is a cavity blade: a blade-sized head with a shallow cavity and a Cut Muscle pad behind the impact area.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno MP-32 ran from 2004 to 2008 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
