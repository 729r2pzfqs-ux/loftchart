About the Mizuno JPX-921 Hot Metal

The JPX-921 Hot Metal moved from the Chromoly 4140M of the JPX-919 to Nickel Chromoly 4335, a stronger alloy that let Mizuno thin the face to 2.0 mm and add a seamless cup-face design. It is the wide-soled, high-offset member of the 2020 JPX family, with offset from 0.224 inches in the 4-iron down to 0.063 inches in the lob wedge, and was sold as a 4-iron through lob wedge set. Lofts are two degrees stronger than the JPX-921 Forged of the same generation: the 7-iron is 29 degrees and the pitching wedge 44 degrees. Lofts, lies and lengths listed are the men's standard steel-shaft build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno JPX-921 Hot Metal 7-iron?

The Mizuno JPX-921 Hot Metal 7-iron has a loft of 29 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

How much stronger are JPX-921 Hot Metal lofts than JPX-921 Forged?

Two degrees through the set. The Hot Metal 7-iron is 29 degrees against 31 degrees for the Forged, and the Hot Metal pitching wedge is 44 degrees against 45 degrees.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Mizuno JPX-921 Hot Metal?

The JPX-921 Hot Metal pitching wedge is 44 degrees. The stock set continues with a 49-degree gap wedge, 54-degree sand wedge and 59-degree lob wedge.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno JPX-921 Hot Metal ran from 2020 to 2022 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
