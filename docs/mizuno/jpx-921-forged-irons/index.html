About the Mizuno JPX-921 Forged

The JPX-921 Forged is a one-piece Grain Flow Forged Chromoly 4120 iron with a face thinned to 2.6 mm and a redesigned sole and topline compared with the JPX-919 Forged. It was sold as a 4-iron through gap wedge set with a players' cavity profile and offset running from 0.150 inches down to 0.091 inches. Lofts were strengthened by one degree through most of the set relative to the JPX-919 Forged, giving a 31-degree 7-iron and a 45-degree pitching wedge, which is still two degrees weaker than the JPX-921 Hot Metal. Lofts, lies and lengths listed are the men's standard steel-shaft build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno JPX-921 Forged 7-iron?

The Mizuno JPX-921 Forged 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

How do JPX-921 Forged lofts compare with JPX-921 Hot Metal?

The Forged is two degrees weaker across the set. Its 7-iron is 31 degrees against 29 degrees for the Hot Metal, and its pitching wedge is 45 degrees against 44 degrees.

Are the JPX-921 Forged irons stronger-lofted than the JPX-919 Forged?

Yes, by one degree through most of the set. The 7-iron went from 32 degrees to 31 degrees and the pitching wedge from 46 degrees to 45 degrees.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno JPX-921 Forged is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
