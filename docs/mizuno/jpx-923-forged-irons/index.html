About the Mizuno JPX-923 Forged

The JPX-923 Forged split the set by material for the first time in the line, using third-generation Chromoly 4120 forging in the 4- through 7-iron for ball speed and 1025E Pure Select mild carbon steel in the 8-iron through gap wedge for feel. Face thickness behind impact dropped to 2.2 mm from 2.6 mm on the JPX-921 Forged, and a new V-Chassis allowed a thinner topline. The stock set is 4-iron through gap wedge with offset from 0.155 inches down to 0.094 inches, and the 7-iron is 30 degrees against 28.5 degrees on the matching JPX-923 Hot Metal. Lofts, lies and lengths listed are the men's standard steel-shaft build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno JPX-923 Forged 7-iron?

The Mizuno JPX-923 Forged 7-iron has a loft of 30 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

How do JPX-923 Forged lofts compare with JPX-923 Hot Metal?

The Forged is weaker by 1.5 degrees through most of the set. Its 7-iron is 30 degrees against 28.5 degrees for the Hot Metal, and its pitching wedge is 44 degrees against 42.5 degrees.

Are the JPX-923 Forged irons made from one material?

No. The 4- through 7-iron are Grain Flow Forged from Chromoly 4120 for ball speed, while the 8-iron through gap wedge are forged from 1025E Pure Select mild carbon steel.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno JPX-923 Forged is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
