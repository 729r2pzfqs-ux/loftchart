About the Mizuno MP-64

The MP-64 replaced the MP-59 as Mizuno's players cavity back and was Grain Flow Forged from a single billet of 1025E mild carbon steel at the Hiroshima plant. It dropped the titanium insert used in the MP-59 in favour of a one-piece forging with a shallower cavity, a thinner topline and slightly less offset. The stock set was 3-PW, running 21 degrees in the 3-iron to 46 degrees in the pitching wedge with a 34-degree 7-iron. Specs below are the men's standard steel-shaft build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno MP-64 7-iron?

The Mizuno MP-64 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Mizuno MP-64?

The MP-64 pitching wedge is 46 degrees, which is around 2 degrees weaker than a typical players-distance pitching wedge of the same era.

Are the Mizuno MP-64 irons forged?

Yes. The MP-64 is Grain Flow Forged from a single billet of 1025E mild carbon steel, with no inserts or multi-material construction.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno MP-64 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
