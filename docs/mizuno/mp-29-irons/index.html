About the Mizuno MP-29

The MP-29 is a Grain Flow Forged muscle-back blade with a curved muscle pad, produced for most of the 1990s and best known as the long-iron half of the combo set Tiger Woods used at the 1997 Masters. It runs stronger than the MP-14 throughout, with a 35-degree 7-iron and a 48-degree pitching wedge, and keeps a 4-degree gap through the short irons. The set was catalogued from a 1-iron through pitching wedge with a matching 56-degree sand wedge. Lengths and lofts listed are the standard men's steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno MP-29 7-iron?

The Mizuno MP-29 7-iron has a loft of 35 degrees, with a 60.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

What year did the Mizuno MP-29 come out?

Mizuno's own irons timeline lists the MP-29 as a 1992 model, produced through 2000. Some spec databases label it 1999 because that is the catalogue year they transcribed.

How do the MP-29 lofts compare with the MP-14?

The MP-29 is roughly 2 degrees stronger through the set: a 35-degree 7-iron and 48-degree pitching wedge against the MP-14's 36 and 50 degrees.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno MP-29 ran from 1992 to 2000 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
