About the Mizuno MP-53

The MP-53 is a Grain Flow Forged players cavity back using a Diamond Muscle shape and a V-shaped milled pocket that removes roughly 14 grams from behind the face and moves it around the cavity. It is the larger and more forgiving of the pair Mizuno launched alongside the MP-63, while keeping a comparatively thin topline and modest offset. Lofts run 21 degrees at the 3-iron to 46 degrees at the pitching wedge, with a 34-degree 7-iron, and the stock set was 3-PW. Lengths and lofts listed are the standard men's steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno MP-53 7-iron?

The Mizuno MP-53 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

How do the MP-53 and MP-63 differ?

They share identical lofts, lies and lengths. The MP-53 is the larger head of the two and carries more offset, 0.142 inches at the 3-iron against 0.122 inches on the MP-63.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Mizuno MP-53?

The MP-53 pitching wedge is 46 degrees at 35.5 inches, one degree stronger than the 47-degree wedge in the earlier MP-52 and MP-62 sets.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno MP-53 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
