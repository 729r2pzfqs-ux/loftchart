About the Mizuno MP-15

The MP-15 sat above the MP-4 as the more forgiving players iron of the pair, reintroducing the titanium muscle insert Mizuno had used in the MP-59 and pairing it with a slightly larger head. Removing mass from the muscle pad allowed weight to be pushed toward the toe and sole while the head stays Grain Flow Forged from 1025E mild carbon steel. The stock 3-PW set shares the MP-4 loft ladder, 21 degrees in the 3-iron to 46 degrees in the pitching wedge with a 34-degree 7-iron, but carries more offset. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno MP-15 7-iron?

The Mizuno MP-15 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Mizuno MP-15?

The MP-15 pitching wedge is 46 degrees, with a 63-degree lie and a 35.5-inch length.

What is the difference between the Mizuno MP-15 and the MP-4?

They share identical lofts, lies and lengths. The MP-15 uses a titanium muscle insert and a slightly larger head with more offset, roughly 0.028 inches more in the 3-iron, making it the more forgiving of the two.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno MP-15 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
