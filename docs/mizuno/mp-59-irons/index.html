About the Mizuno MP-59

The MP-59 is a Grain Flow Forged cavity back that replaced the MP-58 in Mizuno's players line. It uses a titanium insert in the muscle pad, allowing weight to be redistributed to the perimeter for a larger effective hitting area than the MP-58 while keeping a compact head and minimal offset. The set was sold 3-PW with lofts running from 21 degrees in the 3-iron to 46 degrees in the pitching wedge, and a 34-degree 7-iron. Specs below are the men's standard steel-shaft build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno MP-59 7-iron?

The Mizuno MP-59 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Mizuno MP-59?

The MP-59 pitching wedge is 46 degrees, with a 63-degree lie and a 35.5-inch length.

How does the MP-59 differ from the MP-58?

Both share the same lofts and lengths, but the MP-59 adds a titanium insert in the muscle pad so mass can be moved to the perimeter, giving it a larger effective hitting area than the MP-58.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno MP-59 ran from 2011 to 2012 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
