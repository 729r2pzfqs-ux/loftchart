About the Mizuno JPX-919 Hot Metal

The JPX-919 Hot Metal is the cast, distance-oriented member of the 2018 JPX family, built from Chromoly 4140M with a face milled down to 2.0 mm behind impact. It carried a wider sole, a thicker topline and considerably more offset than the JPX-919 Forged, running from 0.230 inches in the 4-iron to 0.102 inches in the wedges. Lofts are materially stronger than the Forged model of the same generation: the 7-iron is 30 degrees and the pitching wedge 45 degrees, against 32 and 46 degrees on the Forged. The stock set ran 4-iron through lob wedge. Lofts, lies and lengths listed are the men's standard steel-shaft build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno JPX-919 Hot Metal 7-iron?

The Mizuno JPX-919 Hot Metal 7-iron has a loft of 30 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

Is the JPX-919 Hot Metal stronger-lofted than the JPX-919 Forged?

Yes. The Hot Metal is two degrees stronger in the 4-, 5-, 6- and 7-irons and one degree stronger from the 8-iron down, so its 30-degree 7-iron sits against the Forged model's 32 degrees.

What wedges were in the JPX-919 Hot Metal set?

The stock range was 4-iron through lob wedge, with a 45-degree pitching wedge, 50-degree gap wedge, 55-degree sand wedge and 60-degree lob wedge.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno JPX-919 Hot Metal is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
