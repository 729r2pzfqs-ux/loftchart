About the Mizuno MP-62

The MP-62 is a Grain Flow Forged players cavity back built around a dual muscle design, with mass split between a low bar and an upper pad to keep the centre of gravity central in a blade-sized head. It has a thin topline, minimal offset and a rounded sole grind, and was played on tour by Luke Donald among others. Lofts run 21 degrees at the 3-iron to 47 degrees at the pitching wedge, with a 35-degree 7-iron, and the stock set was 3-PW. Lengths and lofts listed are the standard men's steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno MP-62 7-iron?

The Mizuno MP-62 7-iron has a loft of 35 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the set makeup of the Mizuno MP-62?

The stock set was 3-iron through pitching wedge, eight clubs, with lofts running from 21 to 47 degrees.

Are the Mizuno MP-62 irons forged?

Yes. The heads are Grain Flow Forged from 1025E mild carbon steel and use a dual muscle back rather than a full cavity.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno MP-62 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
