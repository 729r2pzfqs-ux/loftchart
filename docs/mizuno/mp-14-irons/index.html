About the Mizuno MP-14

The MP-14 is a Grain Flow Forged muscle-back blade from Mizuno's mid-1990s MP line, sold as a long 1-iron through sand wedge range. Its lofts are weak even by the standards of its own era, with a 36-degree 7-iron and a 50-degree pitching wedge, and the loft gap widens to 5 degrees between the 8-iron and the wedges. Lie angles start unusually flat at 55 degrees in the 1-iron. Lengths and lofts listed are the standard men's steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno MP-14 7-iron?

The Mizuno MP-14 7-iron has a loft of 36 degrees, with a 60.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Mizuno MP-14?

The MP-14 pitching wedge is 50 degrees, about 6 to 8 degrees weaker than a modern game-improvement pitching wedge. The stock sand wedge is 56 degrees.

What clubs came in the Mizuno MP-14 set?

The published range runs from a 1-iron through the pitching wedge, with a matching 56-degree sand wedge.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno MP-14 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
