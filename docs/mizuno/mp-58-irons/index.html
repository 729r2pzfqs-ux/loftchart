About the Mizuno MP-58

The MP-58 is a Grain Flow Forged cavity back that uses a titanium muscle insert in place of steel behind the face, freeing weight that Mizuno redistributed to the heel and toe. It is the largest and most forgiving MP iron of its generation and carries the most offset in the range, 0.161 inches at the 3-iron falling to 0.114 inches at the wedge. Lofts follow the traditional MP progression with a 35-degree 7-iron and a 47-degree pitching wedge, and the stock set was 3-PW or 4-PW. Lengths and lofts listed are the standard men's steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno MP-58 7-iron?

The Mizuno MP-58 7-iron has a loft of 35 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the titanium muscle in the Mizuno MP-58?

Mizuno replaced the steel muscle pad behind the face with a lighter titanium insert. The weight saved was moved out to the heel and toe, which is why the MP-58 is the most forgiving iron in that MP range.

How does the MP-58 compare with the MP-63?

The MP-58 is the larger head with a full degree more loft in the mid and short irons - 35 degrees at the 7-iron against 34 on the MP-63 - and roughly 0.04 inches more offset through the long irons.

Buying a set

Mizuno has since introduced the Mizuno MP-59 above it in the range, so the MP-58 is at the end of its retail life rather than fully retired — closeout sets still turn up at retailers while stock lasts, and it is well represented used on eBay, 2nd Swing and Golf Avenue. Check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying a used set: irons are routinely bent from standard during a fitting.
