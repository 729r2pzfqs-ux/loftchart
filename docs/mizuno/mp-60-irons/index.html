About the Mizuno MP-60

The MP-60 is a Grain Flow Forged cavity back built on Mizuno's Cut Muscle design, in which a reinforcing pad sits behind a cavity rather than filling it. Mizuno described the head as about 4 percent larger than the MP-32 it was drawn from, with a correspondingly larger hitting area and slightly more offset. Lofts follow the traditional MP progression, with a 35-degree 7-iron and a 47-degree pitching wedge. The set was sold 3-PW with a 2-iron available; lengths and lofts listed are the standard men's steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno MP-60 7-iron?

The Mizuno MP-60 7-iron has a loft of 35 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

What year did the Mizuno MP-60 come out?

The MP-60 was introduced in 2006 and was replaced by the MP-52 the following year. It is often mislabelled as a 2011 model because it shares a spec chart layout with later MP irons.

How does the MP-60 differ from the MP-32?

The MP-60 uses the same Cut Muscle idea but on a head Mizuno rated as roughly 4 percent larger, and it carries more offset - 0.126 inches at the 7-iron against 0.110 inches on the MP-32 - while keeping identical lofts and lies.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno MP-60 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
