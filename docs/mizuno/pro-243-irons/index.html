About the Mizuno Pro 243

The Mizuno Pro 243 is the players cavity-back of the 2024 Mizuno Pro series, replacing the Pro 223. The 4-7 irons use a 4120 Chromoly face with a wider, longer flow micro slot, while the 8-iron through gap wedge are 1025E Pure Select mild carbon steel Grain Flow Forged HD from a single billet. Compared with the Pro 223 the short irons were strengthened: the 9-iron went from 41 to 40 degrees, the pitching wedge from 46 to 44 and the gap wedge from 51 to 48, while the 7-iron stayed at 32 degrees. Bounce was raised by one degree in the long irons and two in the scoring irons, and the set is right-hand only in 4-GW. Lofts, lies and lengths listed are the men's standard steel-shaft build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno Pro 243 7-iron?

The Mizuno Pro 243 7-iron has a loft of 32 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Mizuno Pro 243?

The Mizuno Pro 243 pitching wedge is 44 degrees and the matching gap wedge is 48 degrees, both 2 to 3 degrees stronger than the Pro 223 they replaced.

Are the Mizuno Pro 243 irons forged?

Yes. The 8-iron through gap wedge are Grain Flow Forged HD from a single billet of 1025E mild carbon steel, and the 4-7 irons combine a forged body with a 4120 Chromoly face and micro slot.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno Pro 243 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
