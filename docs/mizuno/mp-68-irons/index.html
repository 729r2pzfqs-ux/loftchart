About the Mizuno MP-68

The MP-68 is a Grain Flow Forged muscle-back blade with a scalloped muscle pad and a shortened hosel, sold in a 3-iron through pitching wedge range. It runs one degree stronger than the MP-67 it replaced, with a 35-degree 7-iron and a 47-degree pitching wedge, and carries very little offset: 0.110 inches through the 7-iron, tapering to 0.094 inches at the pitching wedge. Bounce climbs from 2 degrees in the long irons to 6 degrees at the wedge. Lengths and lofts listed are the standard men's steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno MP-68 7-iron?

The Mizuno MP-68 7-iron has a loft of 35 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

How much offset do the Mizuno MP-68 irons have?

Very little. Offset is 0.110 inches from the 3-iron through the 7-iron, 0.102 inches in the 8-iron and 9-iron, and 0.094 inches at the pitching wedge.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Mizuno MP-68?

The MP-68 pitching wedge is 47 degrees, with a 63-degree lie, 6 degrees of bounce and a 35.5-inch steel-shaft length.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno MP-68 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
