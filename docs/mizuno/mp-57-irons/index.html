About the Mizuno MP-57

The MP-57 was the most forgiving MP iron of its era, combining Mizuno's Grain Flow Forged construction with a Cut Muscle design in which a reinforcing muscle pad sits behind a full cavity. It carries noticeably more offset than the MP-32 while keeping the same loft progression, and was sold as a 3-PW or 4-PW set. Lengths and lofts listed are the standard men's steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno MP-57 7-iron?

The Mizuno MP-57 7-iron has a loft of 35 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

What makes the MP-57 different from the MP-32?

Both use Mizuno's Cut Muscle design and share the same lofts, but the MP-57 is a fuller cavity back with substantially more offset, making it the more forgiving of the two.

Are the Mizuno MP-57 irons forged?

Yes. They are Grain Flow Forged from a single billet of 1025E mild carbon steel.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno MP-57 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
