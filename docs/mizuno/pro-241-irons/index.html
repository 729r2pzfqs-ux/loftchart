About the Mizuno Pro 241

The Mizuno Pro 241 is the muscle-back of the 2024 Mizuno Pro series and the direct replacement for the Pro 221. It is one-piece Grain Flow Forged HD from 1025E Pure Select mild carbon steel in Hiroshima, with mass gathered centrally behind impact, a soft copper underlay and a full satin brush finish. Lofts carry over unchanged from the Pro 221 at 34 degrees in the 7-iron and 46 degrees in the pitching wedge, but bounce was raised by one degree in the long irons and two degrees in the scoring irons, and the 3-iron lie is half a degree more upright. The stock set is 3-PW in right hand and 4-PW in left. Lofts, lies and lengths listed are the men's standard steel-shaft build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno Pro 241 7-iron?

The Mizuno Pro 241 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

What changed from the Mizuno Pro 221 to the Pro 241?

Lofts, lengths and offset are unchanged. Bounce went up by one degree in the long irons and two degrees in the scoring irons, the 3-iron lie moved from 59 to 59.5 degrees, and the head was retooled with more central mass and a thinner topline.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Mizuno Pro 241?

The Mizuno Pro 241 pitching wedge is 46 degrees. The set runs 3-PW, so gapping below the pitching wedge is covered by separate wedges.

Where it sits in the lineup

Introduced in 2024, the Mizuno Pro 241 is a current blade set in the Mizuno range, with no replacement announced. It replaced the Pro 221. New sets are built to the standard lofts, lies and lengths in the chart above, though a fitting can move any of them — so confirm the numbers on the order sheet. Used sets appear on eBay, 2nd Swing and Golf Avenue, and should be measured against this chart before buying.
