About the Mizuno JPX-900 Forged

The JPX-900 Forged is Grain Flow Forged from a boron-infused 1025E carbon steel, which Mizuno used to build a thinner, stronger face while keeping a bendable hosel for loft and lie adjustment. It is a compact players-distance iron with a modest cavity and moderate offset, sold as a 4-PW plus gap wedge set. Lofts are stronger than Mizuno's MP line of the same period, with a 31-degree 7-iron. Lengths and lofts listed are the standard men's steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Mizuno JPX-900 Forged 7-iron?

The Mizuno JPX-900 Forged 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the JPX-900 Forged made of?

It is Grain Flow Forged from 1025E carbon steel infused with boron, which lets Mizuno use a thinner, stronger face while keeping the hosel bendable for loft and lie adjustment.

How strong are the JPX-900 Forged lofts?

They are stronger than Mizuno's MP irons of the same era: 31 degrees at the 7-iron and 45 degrees at the pitching wedge, versus 35 and 47 degrees on the MP-57.

Finding a used set

The Mizuno JPX-900 Forged is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
