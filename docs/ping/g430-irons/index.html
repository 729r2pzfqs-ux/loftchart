About the Ping G430

The G430 strengthened the set by a full degree at the 7-iron, to 29 degrees, and restructured the bottom of the bag: the pitching wedge is 41 degrees and Ping fills the gap with loft-numbered 45 and 50 degree wedges rather than a single utility wedge. A PurFlex cavity badge replaces the earlier damping insert. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping G430 7-iron?

The Ping G430 7-iron has a loft of 29 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.0 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 37.0 inches.

When were Ping G430 irons made?

The G430 was introduced in 2023 and was replaced by the G440 in 2025.

What wedges come with the Ping G430 irons?

The G430 pitching wedge is 41 degrees, followed by loft-numbered 45 and 50 degree wedges, then a 54-degree sand wedge and a 58-degree lob wedge.

Finding a used set

The Ping G430 ran from 2023 to 2025 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
