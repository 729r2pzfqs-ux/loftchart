About the Ping G

Ping named the 2016 generation simply G. Lofts, lengths, offsets and bounce carry over from the G30 unchanged, with the 7-iron at 30.5 degrees; the change is the COR-Eye face technology and slightly revised lie angles through the set. The sand and lob wedge swing weights are lighter than the G30's. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping G 7-iron?

The Ping G 7-iron has a loft of 30.5 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.0 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 37.0 inches.

When were Ping G irons made?

The G was introduced in 2016 and was replaced by the G400 in 2017.

What is the difference between the Ping G30 and G irons?

Lofts, lengths, offset and bounce are identical. The G adds the COR-Eye face, revises lie angles slightly and uses lighter wedge swing weights.

Finding a used set

The Ping G is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
