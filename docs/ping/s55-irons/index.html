About the Ping S55

The S55 was the last of Ping's counting-down S series and the first of them to move off the long-running S57/S56 loft chart: the 7-iron is 33 degrees rather than 34.25, and the 6- and 8-irons move to round numbers, giving even four-degree gaps from the 4-iron down. Offset is cut slightly against the S56, to 0.09 inches at the 7-iron, and a tungsten toe weight stabilises the head. The set runs 3-iron through pitching wedge at D1 swing weight. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping S55 7-iron?

The Ping S55 7-iron has a loft of 33 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.25 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 36.75 inches.

When were Ping S55 irons made?

The S55 was introduced in 2013, following the S56. It was the last model in Ping's S series — no S54 followed, and the tour-players role passed to the iBlade in 2016.

What is the difference between the Ping S56 and S55?

The S55 is 1.25 degrees stronger at the 7-iron (33 against 34.25) and a degree or more stronger through the mid and short irons, with slightly less offset at the 3-iron. Lies and lengths are unchanged.

How much offset does the Ping S55 have?

0.17 inches at the 3-iron, 0.09 at the 7-iron and 0.03 at the pitching wedge.

Finding a used set

The Ping S55 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
