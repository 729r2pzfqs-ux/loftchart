About the Ping Anser Forged

The Anser Forged was Ping's first modern forged iron, built from 8620 carbon steel with a tungsten toe weight and a Custom Tuning Port. It runs 3-iron through pitching wedge with a 33.5-degree 7-iron — half a degree stronger than the cast S56 of the same era — and holds a D1 swing weight through the irons. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping Anser Forged 7-iron?

The Ping Anser Forged 7-iron has a loft of 33.5 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.25 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 36.75 inches.

When were Ping Anser Forged irons made?

The Anser Forged was introduced in 2012 and was succeeded by the Anser V2.

Was the Ping Anser Forged Ping's first forged iron?

It was Ping's first modern forged iron, made from 8620 carbon steel rather than the investment-cast stainless Ping had used across its earlier lines.

Finding a used set

The Ping Anser Forged is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
