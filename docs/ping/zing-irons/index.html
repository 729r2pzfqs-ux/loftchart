About the Ping Zing

The Zing replaced the Eye 2 as Ping's mainstream game-improvement iron and moved the line about 1.5 degrees stronger through the middle of the set — the 7-iron went from 36 degrees in the Eye 2 to 34.5 degrees here. Ping did not publish per-club offset or bounce figures for the Zing, so those columns are omitted rather than guessed. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. The lies below are the BLACK (standard) lies.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping Zing 7-iron?

The Ping Zing 7-iron has a loft of 34.5 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 63.5 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 36.75 inches.

When were Ping Zing irons made?

The Zing was introduced in 1992 and was replaced by the Zing 2 in 1994.

Are Ping Zing lofts stronger than the Eye 2?

Yes, by roughly 1.5 degrees through the middle irons. The Zing 7-iron is 34.5 degrees against 36 degrees for the Eye 2.

Finding a used set

The Ping Zing is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
