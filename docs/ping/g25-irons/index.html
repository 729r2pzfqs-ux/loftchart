About the Ping G25

The G25 refined the G-series formula with a Custom Tuning Port and progressive geometry: wider soles and more offset in the long irons for higher launch, thinner soles and less offset in the scoring clubs for control. Lofts are identical to the 2009 G15 through the set, with the 7-iron at 32 degrees, but bounce was softened in the long irons (the 3-iron moved from -2 to 0 degrees). Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. The lies below are the BLACK (standard) lies.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping G25 7-iron?

The Ping G25 7-iron has a loft of 32 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.25 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 36.75 inches.

Are Ping G25 lofts stronger than the G15?

No — the two sets share identical lofts, lies and lengths through the bag. The G25 differs mainly in sole width, offset profile and bounce, which was reduced in the long irons.

What shafts came stock in the Ping G25 irons?

Ping CFS steel in SR, R, S and X flexes (94-114 grams) or Ping TFC 189I graphite in SR, R and S flexes (70-86 grams).

Finding a used set

The Ping G25 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
