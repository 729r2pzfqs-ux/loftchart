About the Ping Eye 2

The Eye 2 is the best-selling iron model in golf history and the club that established Ping's perimeter-weighted, heel-toe balanced cavity back as the standard game-improvement shape. Lofts are traditional by modern standards — the 7-iron sits at 36 degrees, roughly four degrees weaker than a 2010s game-improvement 7-iron. Ping fitted the Eye 2 with its colour-code (dot) system, in which the coloured dot in the cavity denotes the lie angle relative to standard: gold is 4 degrees flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK is standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright and maroon 5 upright. The lie angles listed below are the BLACK (standard) lies. The square-groove Eye 2 was also the subject of the long-running USGA/PGA Tour groove dispute of the late 1980s.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping Eye 2 7-iron?

The Ping Eye 2 7-iron has a loft of 36 degrees, with a standard (black dot) lie of 62.5 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 36.75 inches.

What does the black dot on a Ping Eye 2 mean?

Black is Ping's standard lie angle. Other dot colours shift the lie flatter (gold, brown, orange, red) or more upright (blue, green, white, silver, maroon) in one-degree steps.

When were Ping Eye 2 irons made?

The Eye 2 was introduced in September 1982 and remained in the line until 1993, when the Zing had taken over as Ping's mainstream game-improvement iron. It is the best-selling iron model in golf history.

Are Ping Eye 2 irons conforming?

Square-groove Eye 2 irons manufactured before 1 April 1990 remain conforming for general play under the terms of Ping's 1990 settlement with the USGA and the PGA Tour, which grandfathered those clubs. That settlement stayed in force when the tighter groove rule took effect in 2010, briefly making pre-1990 Eye 2 wedges a talking point on tour. Later Eye 2 production is subject to the normal groove rules.

Are Ping Eye 2 lofts weak compared to modern irons?

Yes. The Eye 2 7-iron is 36 degrees, whereas the Ping G25 7-iron from 2013 is 32 degrees — four degrees stronger, or roughly one club of difference.

Finding a used set

The Ping Eye 2 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
