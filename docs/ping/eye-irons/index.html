About the Ping Eye

The Eye was the first Ping iron to use the eye-shaped cavity that gave the family its name, replacing the Karsten models with a more compact head, more weight pushed to the perimeter and progressive hosel offset and sole camber through the set. It lasted only four years before the longer-bladed, thinner-toplined Eye 2 replaced it in September 1982 and went on to become the best-selling iron in golf. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. The lies below are the BLACK (standard) lies, and they are identical to the pre-1998 Eye 2 chart — see the note in sources, because the numbers here rest on a single secondary table and are the least well corroborated figures in this Ping set.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping Eye 7-iron?

The Ping Eye 7-iron is listed at 36 degrees, with a standard (black dot) lie of 62.5 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 36.75 inches. Only one secondary spec table for this model could be found, so treat the figure as indicative rather than definitive.

When were Ping Eye irons made?

The Eye was introduced in 1978 and ran until September 1982, when the Eye 2 replaced it. Some sources date the Eye to 1979.

What is the difference between the Ping Eye and the Eye 2?

The Eye 2 has a longer blade, a taller face, a thinner top line and a refined eye-shaped cavity. Published lofts, lies and lengths for the Eye match the pre-1998 Eye 2 chart, so the change was in head shape and forgiveness rather than in the loft progression.

What does the black dot on a Ping Eye mean?

Black is Ping's standard lie angle. The other dot colours shift the lie flatter (gold, brown, orange, red) or more upright (blue, green, white, silver, maroon) in one-degree steps.

Finding a used set

The Ping Eye is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
