About the Ping ISI

The ISI replaced the Zing 2 as Ping's mainstream cavity-back iron in 1998 and was offered alongside the larger, more forgiving ISI-K. Lofts are noticeably stronger than the Eye 2 of a decade earlier — the 7-iron moved from 36 to 34.5 degrees — while lie angles became more upright. Ping offered an unusually deep wedge matrix for the set: W (47), W2 (49.5), S (52), S2 (54.5), S3 (57) and L (61); the table below lists W, W2, S and L. As with all Ping irons of the era, the coloured dot in the cavity encodes lie angle relative to standard — gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping ISI 7-iron?

The Ping ISI 7-iron has a loft of 34.5 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 63.5 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 36.75 inches.

What wedges came with the Ping ISI set?

Ping offered six wedge options: W at 47 degrees, W2 at 49.5, S at 52, S2 at 54.5, S3 at 57 and L at 61 degrees.

What replaced the Ping ISI?

The Ping i3 O-Size took over as Ping's mainstream game-improvement iron in 2000.

Finding a used set

The Ping ISI is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
