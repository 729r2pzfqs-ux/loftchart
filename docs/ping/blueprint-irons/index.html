About the Ping Blueprint

The Blueprint is Ping's tour blade: a one-piece 8620 carbon steel forging with a tungsten toe screw, built from 2-iron through pitching wedge. Offset is the tightest in the Ping range at 0.05 inches in the 7-iron, and lofts are traditional with that club at 34 degrees — four degrees weaker than the G410 of the same year. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping Blueprint 7-iron?

The Ping Blueprint 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.0 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 36.75 inches.

When were Ping Blueprint irons made?

The Blueprint was introduced in 2019, following the iBlade, and was succeeded by the Blueprint T and Blueprint S.

How much offset does the Ping Blueprint have?

It has the least offset of any Ping iron: 0.14 inches at the 2-iron, 0.05 at the 7-iron and 0.02 at the pitching wedge.

Buying a set

Ping has since introduced the Ping Blueprint T above it in the range, so the Blueprint is at the end of its retail life rather than fully retired — closeout sets still turn up at retailers while stock lasts, and it is well represented used on eBay, 2nd Swing and Golf Avenue. Check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying a used set: irons are routinely bent from standard during a fitting.
