About the Ping i230

The i230 is a multi-material cast players iron: a 431 stainless body with tungsten toe and tip weights, a multi-part cavity badge and a concealed activated elastomer insert, plus tighter-spaced MicroMax grooves. It keeps the i210's loft, lie and length chart exactly — the 7-iron is 33 degrees at 62.0 lie and 37 inches — but shortens the blade in the 3 to 5 irons and trims offset there, from 0.21 to 0.20 at the 3-iron and 0.15 to 0.14 at the 5-iron. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. The lies below are the BLACK (standard) lies.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping i230 7-iron?

The Ping i230 7-iron has a loft of 33 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.0 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 37.0 inches.

When were Ping i230 irons made?

The i230 was announced in 2022, following the i210, and reached retail in early 2023.

What is the difference between the Ping i210 and the i230?

Lofts, lies and lengths are identical club for club. The i230 shortens the blade in the 3 to 5 irons, trims offset there by 0.01 inches at each of those clubs, adds tungsten tip weighting alongside the toe weight, and switches to MicroMax grooves.

How strong are Ping i230 lofts?

Traditional for a players iron. The 7-iron is 33 degrees against 29 for the game-improvement G430 of the same period — four degrees, or roughly one club, weaker.

Finding a used set

The Ping i230 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
