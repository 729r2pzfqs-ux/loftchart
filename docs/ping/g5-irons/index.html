About the Ping G5

The G5 followed the G2 and kept the same 34-degree 7-iron, but published a full set of per-club offset and bounce figures. Offset runs progressively from 0.32 inches at the 2-iron down to 0.15 at the lob wedge, and bounce climbs from -3 degrees to 14 across the same span. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping G5 7-iron?

The Ping G5 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.28 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 36.75 inches.

When were Ping G5 irons made?

The G5 was introduced in 2005 and was replaced by the G10 in 2007.

How much offset do Ping G5 irons have?

Offset is progressive: 0.32 inches at the 2-iron, 0.25 at the 7-iron and 0.15 at the lob wedge.

Finding a used set

The Ping G5 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
