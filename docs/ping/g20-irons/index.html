About the Ping G20

The G20 moved the G-series two degrees stronger at the 7-iron, from 34 degrees in the G15 to 32, and dropped the 2-iron from the stock line-up. The set uses a Custom Tuning Port to shift mass low and back. Published bounce figures were not part of the archived G20 chart and are omitted here. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping G20 7-iron?

The Ping G20 7-iron has a loft of 32 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.25 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 36.75 inches.

When were Ping G20 irons made?

The G20 was introduced in 2011 and was replaced by the G25 in 2013.

Are Ping G20 lofts stronger than the G15?

Yes, by two degrees through most of the set. The G20 7-iron is 32 degrees against 34 for the G15.

Finding a used set

The Ping G20 ran from 2011 to 2013 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
