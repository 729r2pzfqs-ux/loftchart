About the Ping i200

The i200 is a cast 431 stainless players iron with a thinned face, a deeper Custom Tuning Port and weight moved to the hosel region and toe to raise MOI. Its loft, lie and length chart carried over unchanged into the i210 that replaced it a year later — the 7-iron is 33 degrees at 62.0 lie and 37 inches — but the i200 runs slightly more offset through the long irons, 0.19 inches at the 4-iron and 0.17 at the 5-iron against the i210's 0.18 and 0.15. The set is 3-iron through pitching wedge plus a 50-degree utility wedge. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping i200 7-iron?

The Ping i200 7-iron has a loft of 33 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.0 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 37.0 inches.

When were Ping i200 irons made?

The i200 was introduced in 2017 and was replaced by the i210 in 2018 — one of the shortest runs of any Ping iron.

What is the difference between the Ping i200 and the i210?

Lofts, lies and lengths are identical club for club. The i210 adds a softer elastomer insert in the Custom Tuning Port and trims the long-iron offset slightly, from 0.19 to 0.18 inches at the 4-iron and 0.17 to 0.15 at the 5-iron.

What clubs come in a Ping i200 set?

3-iron through pitching wedge, plus a 50-degree utility wedge that shares the pitching wedge's 64.1-degree lie and 35.5-inch length.

Finding a used set

The Ping i200 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.

Across every archived snapshot checked, the i200 6-iron repeats the 7-iron's lie (62.0), offset (0.09) and bounce (9.0) verbatim, which cannot be right for a set whose other rows follow Ping's standard 0.7-0.8 degree lie progression. The 6-iron lie of 61.3 recorded here comes from the qsponge.com i200 spec table, which matches Ping's chart on every other club and matches the identical 59.0 / 59.8 / 60.5 / 61.3 / 62.0 / 62.8 / 63.5 / 64.1 progression Ping publishes for the iBlade, i210, i230 and i525. The 6-iron offset and bounce are deliberately OMITTED: qsponge gives 0.14 and 8.0, but that is a single secondary source for a row already known to be corrupt in the manufacturer chart, so those keys are left out rather than guessed.
