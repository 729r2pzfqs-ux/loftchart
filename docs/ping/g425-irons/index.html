About the Ping G425

The G425 is dimensionally identical to the G410 — every published loft, length, lie, offset, bounce and swing weight matches through the set, with the 7-iron at 30 degrees. The changes are to the face, which moves to a variable-thickness design, rather than to the geometry. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping G425 7-iron?

The Ping G425 7-iron has a loft of 30 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.0 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 37.0 inches.

When were Ping G425 irons made?

The G425 was introduced in 2021 and was replaced by the G430 in 2023.

What is the difference between the Ping G410 and G425 irons?

The published specifications are identical through the set — same lofts, lies, lengths, offsets, bounce and swing weights. The G425 differs in face construction.

Finding a used set

The Ping G425 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
