About the Ping G15

The G15 is a wide-soled, high-launch game-improvement iron with a Custom Tuning Port and heavily progressive offset — 0.32 inches in the 3-iron tapering to 0.10 inches in the lob wedge. Lofts are strong for the era: the 7-iron is 32 degrees, four degrees stronger than the 1980s Ping Eye 2 and 2.3 degrees stronger than the 2003 G2. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. The lies below are the BLACK (standard) lies. Graphite-shafted clubs run one swing-weight point lighter than the steel values shown.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping G15 7-iron?

The Ping G15 7-iron has a loft of 32 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.25 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 36.75 inches.

What are the Ping G15 wedge lofts?

The pitching wedge is 45 degrees, the utility wedge 50, the sand wedge 54 and the lob wedge 58 degrees.

How much offset do Ping G15 irons have?

Offset is progressive, running from 0.32 inches in the 3-iron down to 0.10 inches in the lob wedge, with 0.24 inches at the 7-iron.

Finding a used set

The Ping G15 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
