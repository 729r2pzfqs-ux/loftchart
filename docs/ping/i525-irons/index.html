About the Ping i525

The i525 replaced the i500 as Ping's hollow-body players-distance iron, pairing a forged maraging steel face with a 17-4 stainless body, an internal sole undercut and tungsten weighting. It keeps the i500's lofts, lies and lengths exactly — the 7-iron is 30.5 degrees at 62.0 lie and 37 inches, four degrees stronger than the i230 of the same era — while cutting offset in the mid and short irons, to 0.08 inches at the 7-iron. The set runs 3-iron through pitching wedge plus a 50-degree utility wedge. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping i525 7-iron?

The Ping i525 7-iron has a loft of 30.5 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.0 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 37.0 inches.

When were Ping i525 irons made?

The i525 was announced in February 2022 and reached retail in March that year, replacing the i500.

What is the difference between the Ping i500 and the i525?

Lofts, lies and lengths are identical club for club. The i525 changes the body to 17-4 stainless with an internal sole undercut, adds tungsten weighting, and reduces offset through the mid and short irons — 0.08 inches at the 7-iron against the i500's 0.09, and 0.02 at the 9-iron against 0.05.

Is the Ping i525 a distance iron?

Yes. It is a hollow-body players-distance model. Its 7-iron is 30.5 degrees, 2.5 degrees stronger than the players-iron i230 of the same period and 1.5 degrees weaker than the game-improvement G430.

Finding a used set

The Ping i525 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
