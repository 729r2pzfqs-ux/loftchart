About the Ping G2

The G2 launched the G-series that has been Ping's game-improvement line ever since; it reached most retail shelves for the 2004 season. It kept the Custom Tuning Port of the i3 while widening the sole and adding progressive offset, and it was also sold in G2 EZ (slower swing speed), G2 HL (higher loft) and G2 L (ladies) variants. Lofts are only marginally stronger than the i3 O-Size, with the 7-iron at 34.3 degrees. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping G2 7-iron?

The Ping G2 7-iron has a loft of 34.3 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.28 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 36.75 inches.

When were the Ping G2 irons released?

Ping introduced the G2 in 2003 and it was the company's headline game-improvement iron through the 2004 season, before the G5 replaced it in 2005.

What variants of the Ping G2 iron were available?

Besides the standard G2, Ping offered the G2 EZ for slower swing speeds, the G2 HL with higher lofts and the G2 L ladies model.

Finding a used set

The Ping G2 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
