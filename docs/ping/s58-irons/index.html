About the Ping S58

The S58 is the middle model of Ping's counting-down S series, a compact cast players iron with a dual-depth cavity, a tuning pocket and a vertical stabilising bar. Ping moved the centre of gravity progressively through the set — further back in the long irons for a higher launch, further forward in the short irons for a flatter, more controlled flight. It runs 2-iron through pitching wedge with the 7-iron at 34.25 degrees and only 0.09 inches of offset, and the long irons carry negative bounce. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping S58 7-iron?

The Ping S58 7-iron has a loft of 34.25 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.28 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 36.75 inches.

When were Ping S58 irons made?

The S58 was introduced in 2006 and was replaced by the S57 in 2007. It sits between the S59 of 2003 and the S57 in Ping's counting-down S series.

What is the difference between the Ping S58 and the S57?

The two share the 34.25-degree 7-iron and 0.09 inches of offset at that club, but the S57 rounds its lies to the nearest quarter degree (62.25 at the 7-iron against the S58's 62.28) and adds a tungsten toe weight.

How much offset does the Ping S58 have?

Very little: 0.21 inches at the 2-iron falling to 0.09 at the 7-iron and 0.04 at the pitching wedge.

Finding a used set

The Ping S58 ran from 2006 to 2007 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
