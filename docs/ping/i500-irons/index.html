About the Ping i500

The i500 was Ping's first hollow-body players-distance iron, pairing a thin maraging steel face with a compact forged body. It launched alongside the i210 and is four degrees stronger at the 7-iron — 30.5 against 33 — while keeping players-iron offset of 0.09 inches at the same club. Ping's colour-code (dot) system sets lie angle relative to standard: gold 4 flat, brown 3 flat, orange 2 flat, red 1 flat, BLACK standard, blue 1 upright, green 2 upright, white 3 upright, silver 4 upright, maroon 5 upright. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Ping i500 7-iron?

The Ping i500 7-iron has a loft of 30.5 degrees, a standard (black dot) lie of 62.0 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 37.0 inches.

When were Ping i500 irons made?

The i500 was introduced in 2018 and was succeeded by the i525.

What is the difference between the Ping i210 and i500?

The i500 is a hollow-body distance iron, 2.5 degrees stronger at the 7-iron (30.5 against 33) with the same 0.09 inches of offset at that club.

Finding a used set

The Ping i500 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
