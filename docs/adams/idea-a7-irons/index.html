About the Adams Idea a7

The Idea a7 is a hybrid-iron combo set: the stock eight-club make-up replaces the long irons with a 3-hybrid and 4-hybrid, then runs a hollow-bodied 5-iron and conventional dual-cavity short irons from the 6-iron down. The 5-iron and 5-hybrid were built to the same 25-degree loft, 60.5-degree lie and 38.5-inch length so the two could be swapped without a gap in the set. Lofts are strong for the period, with a 33-degree 7-iron and a 45-degree pitching wedge, and matching GW, SW and LW were offered to extend the set. Iron sets began shipping on 1 August 2009 at 599 USD in steel and 699 USD in graphite. Only the iron clubs are listed below; the 3-hybrid (19 degrees, 59-degree lie, 40 inches) and 4-hybrid (22 degrees, 59.75-degree lie, 39.25 inches) are part of the stock set but are hybrids rather than irons.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of an Adams Idea a7 7-iron?

The Adams Idea a7 7-iron has a loft of 33 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37.25-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a stock Adams Idea a7 set?

The stock set is a hybrid-iron combo: a 3-hybrid and 4-hybrid replace the long irons, followed by a hollow-body 5-iron and cavity-back 6-iron through pitching wedge. Matching GW, SW and LW were sold to extend the set.

Is the Adams Idea a7 5-iron the same loft as the 5-hybrid?

Yes. Adams built both to 25 degrees with a 60.5-degree lie and a 38.5-inch length so players could swap one for the other without changing the set gapping.

Finding a used set

The Adams Idea a7 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
