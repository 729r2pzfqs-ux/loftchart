About the Adams XTD

The XTD is the game-improvement iron of Adams' 2014 XTD family, sold alongside the smaller XTD Forged and XTD Tour models and built around a Cross Cavity head with the brand's thinnest cast face to that point. A Cut-Thru sole slot roughly 25 percent longer than the previous generation runs through the sole of the long and mid irons to raise ball speed low on the face. Unlike the Idea sets it is a conventional all-iron set rather than a hybrid combo, offered from the 3-iron down with GW and SW to extend it. Lofts are strong for the era, with a 31-degree 7-iron and a 44-degree pitching wedge, and the stock steel shaft was the KBS C-Taper 90. It was one of the last iron models released under the Adams name before TaylorMade retired the brand.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of an Adams XTD 7-iron?

The Adams XTD 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37.25-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a stock Adams XTD set?

The XTD is a conventional iron set rather than a hybrid combo. Heads ran from the 3-iron through the pitching wedge, with a 49-degree gap wedge and 54-degree sand wedge available to extend the set.

How strong are Adams XTD lofts compared with rivals of the time?

Moderately strong but not extreme for 2014. The 28-degree 6-iron was about 1.5 degrees weaker than the TaylorMade SpeedBlade and about 2 degrees weaker than the Callaway X2 Hot.

Finding a used set

The Adams XTD is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
