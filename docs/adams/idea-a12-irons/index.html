About the Adams Idea a12

This is the a12-generation hybrid-iron set that followed the Idea a7, announced on 16 September 2011 and sold by Adams as the Idea a12 OS; the name Idea a12 on its own belonged to the matching standalone hybrid, offered in 17, 19, 22 and 25 degrees, and the players' iron of the same generation was the separate forged Idea Pro a12. The eight-club stock set is heavily hybrid-weighted: 4, 5 and 6 are full hybrids carrying Adams' Velocity Slot Technology, 7 and 8 are truss-back Transitional Hybrids, and only the 9-iron, pitching wedge and gap wedge are conventional deep undercut cavity-back irons. Lofts run in a 4-degree ladder with a 32-degree 7-iron and a 44-degree pitching wedge. Listed below are the iron-shaped clubs from the 7 down, plus the 54-degree SW and 59-degree LW heads Adams offered separately; the 4, 5 and 6 hybrids (22, 25 and 28 degrees) are part of the stock set but are hybrids rather than irons. The men's set was 699.99 USD in steel and 799.99 USD in graphite and reached shops in early October 2011.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of an Adams Idea a12 7-iron?

32 degrees, with a 62.25-degree lie and a 37.5-inch length. Note that the 7 in this set is a Transitional Hybrid rather than a conventional iron.

What clubs come in a stock Adams Idea a12 set?

Eight clubs, 4 through GW. The 4, 5 and 6 are Velocity Slot hybrids, the 7 and 8 are truss-back Transitional Hybrids, and the 9, pitching wedge and gap wedge are cavity-back irons. Separate 54-degree SW and 59-degree LW heads were also available.

Is the Adams Idea a12 the same thing as the Idea a12 OS?

Yes, for iron sets. Adams sold the a12-generation hybrid-iron set as the Idea a12 OS; the name Idea a12 on its own referred to the matching standalone hybrid, made in 17, 19, 22 and 25 degrees. The forged players' iron of the same generation was a different club, the Idea Pro a12, whose 7-iron is 34 degrees rather than 32.

Finding a used set

The Adams Idea a12 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
