About the Srixon ZX7

The ZX7 is the players model in Srixon's first ZX iron line, sitting above the ZX5 and ZX4. The head is forged from 1020 carbon steel, with tungsten in the toe of the 3-iron through 7-iron to raise MOI without widening the profile, and it carries the narrowest sole and least offset in the line. Lofts are traditional for a players iron, with a 32-degree 7-iron and a 46-degree pitching wedge, and lengths and lies match the rest of the ZX line. Individual heads run 3-iron through a 51-degree approach wedge, with 3-PW in right and left hand and the approach wedge right hand only. Offsets below are converted from Srixon's published millimetre figures (for example the 7-iron is 2.9 mm, or 0.114 inches).

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Srixon ZX7 7-iron?

The Srixon ZX7 7-iron has a loft of 32 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a stock Srixon ZX7 set?

Heads run 3-iron through a 51-degree approach wedge. The 3-iron through pitching wedge are offered in both right and left hand; the approach wedge is right hand only.

How much offset does the Srixon ZX7 have?

Srixon lists 2.9 mm (0.114 inches) of offset at the 7-iron, ranging from 3.6 mm (0.142 inches) in the 3-iron down to 1.4 mm (0.055 inches) in the approach wedge — the least offset in the ZX line.

Finding a used set

The Srixon ZX7 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
