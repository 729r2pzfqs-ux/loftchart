About the Srixon ZX5 Mk II

The ZX5 Mk II is the distance model in Srixon's second ZX iron generation, announced on 11 January 2023 and sitting between the ZX7 Mk II and the ZX4 Mk II. It carries the second generation of Srixon's MainFrame technology, a variable-thickness pattern of grooves, channels and cavities milled into the back of the face to maximise flex at impact and shift mass to the toe and sole. Lofts carry over unchanged from the original ZX5, with a 31-degree 7-iron and a 44-degree pitching wedge, and lies and lengths match the rest of the ZX Mk II line. Heads run 3-iron through a 50-degree approach wedge, with 4-AW the usual stock set. Offsets below are converted from Srixon's published millimetre figures (for example the 7-iron is 3.2 mm, or 0.126 inches); the 3-iron and approach wedge offsets are omitted because Srixon's per-club table for this head covers 4-iron through pitching wedge only.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Srixon ZX5 Mk II 7-iron?

The Srixon ZX5 Mk II 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a stock Srixon ZX5 Mk II set?

Heads run 3-iron through a 50-degree approach wedge, with 4-iron through approach wedge the usual stock set.

Did the lofts change between the Srixon ZX5 and the ZX5 Mk II?

No. The ZX5 Mk II carries over the ZX5 lofts unchanged, from a 20-degree 3-iron through a 31-degree 7-iron to a 50-degree approach wedge.

Finding a used set

The Srixon ZX5 Mk II is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
