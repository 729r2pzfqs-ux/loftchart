About the Srixon ZX4

The ZX4 is the most forgiving model in Srixon's first ZX iron line, added a season after the ZX5 and ZX7. It uses a hollow-body construction with a 431 stainless steel body, a forged HT1770 high-strength steel face and Srixon's MainFrame milled pattern behind the face, plus tungsten in the sole of the 4-iron through 7-iron to lower the centre of gravity. Lofts are the strongest in the ZX line, with a 28.5-degree 7-iron and a 43-degree pitching wedge, while lies and lengths match the ZX5 and ZX7. The stock set is eight clubs, 4-iron through a 49-degree approach wedge, with 4-PW in right and left hand and the approach wedge right hand only. Offsets below are converted from Srixon's published millimetre figures (for example the 7-iron is 3.7 mm, or 0.146 inches).

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Srixon ZX4 7-iron?

The Srixon ZX4 7-iron has a loft of 28.5 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a stock Srixon ZX4 set?

The stock set is eight clubs, 4-iron through a 49-degree approach wedge. The 4-iron through pitching wedge are offered in both right and left hand; the approach wedge is right hand only.

Are the Srixon ZX4 irons hollow?

Yes. The ZX4 is a hollow-body iron with a 431 stainless steel body, a forged HT1770 high-strength steel face and tungsten in the sole of the 4-iron through 7-iron.

Finding a used set

The Srixon ZX4 ran from 2021 to 2023 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
