About the Srixon Z 765

The Z 765 is the muscle-cavity players model in Srixon's 2016 three-model Z Series iron line, sitting between the Z 565 cavity back and the Z 965 blade. Srixon describes it as ultra-soft 1020 carbon steel combined with a new special heat treatment and precision forged, with the revised Tour V.T. sole and grooves five percent larger than the previous Z 745 generation. Lofts are traditional for a players iron, with a 32-degree 7-iron and a 46-degree pitching wedge, and offset is minimal — 0.104 inches at the 7-iron against 0.128 inches on the Z 565. Those lofts carry over unchanged into the Z 785 that replaced it. The stock range is 3-iron through pitching wedge in right and left hand, with a 51-degree approach wedge in right hand only, and the set is offered in steel only. Srixon published this model's offset figures directly in inches, so no unit conversion was applied. The Tour V.T. sole is listed with separate leading- and trailing-edge bounce figures rather than a single bounce number, so bounce is omitted here. Srixon announced the club in July 2016 and it reached retail on 16 September 2016, so it is often catalogued as a 2017 model.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Srixon Z 765 7-iron?

The Srixon Z 765 7-iron has a loft of 32 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a stock Srixon Z 765 set?

The stock range is 3-iron through pitching wedge, available in both right and left hand, plus a 51-degree approach wedge offered in right hand only. The set was offered in steel shafts only.

How much offset do the Srixon Z 765 irons have?

Very little for the category. Srixon lists 0.104 inches of offset at the 7-iron, falling to 0.073 inches at the pitching wedge, against 0.128 and 0.089 inches on the more forgiving Z 565.

Did the lofts change between the Srixon Z 765 and the Z 785?

No. The Z 785 carries over the Z 765 lofts unchanged, from a 20-degree 3-iron through a 32-degree 7-iron to a 51-degree approach wedge.

Finding a used set

The Srixon Z 765 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
