About the Srixon ZXi7

The ZXi7 is the players model in Srixon's ZXi iron line, which replaced the ZX Mk II generation. It is built with Srixon's i-Forged process: Condensed Forging inserts extra strength in the hosel area, which Srixon says is what allows it to use the softer S15C steel in a single-piece body — a head it describes as 7 percent softer than the previous generation, the softest steel it has used in an iron. Behind the sweet spot sits an enlarged forged PureFrame ridge, and tungsten weighting sets the centre of gravity. Lofts carry over unchanged from the ZX7 Mk II, with a 32-degree 7-iron and a 46-degree pitching wedge, and offset is the least in the line at 2.8 mm in the 7-iron. The published lengths look a quarter-inch longer than the ZX7 Mk II, but they are not: Srixon changed how it measures and publishes iron length to match USGA and R&A industry guidelines, and states directly that the actual length of the irons has not changed. The figures below are Srixon's published ZXi-convention lengths, so comparing them with the pre-ZXi models in this database will overstate the difference by about a quarter inch. Heads run 3-iron through a 57-degree sand wedge, with 4-PW and 5-PW the stock sets and the 3-iron and approach wedge available individually. Offsets are converted from Srixon's published millimetre figures (the 7-iron is 2.8 mm, or 0.110 inches). Srixon unveiled the line in late 2024 and it reached retail in January 2025, so it is commonly catalogued as a 2025 model.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Srixon ZXi7 7-iron?

The Srixon ZXi7 7-iron has a loft of 32 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a published steel-shaft length of 37.25 inches.

Why is the Srixon ZXi7 7-iron listed at 37.25 inches when the ZX7 Mk II was 37 inches?

Only the measuring convention changed. Srixon altered how it measures and publishes iron length to match USGA and R&A industry guidelines, which added about a quarter inch to the published figures, and states that the actual length of the irons has not changed.

What clubs come in a stock Srixon ZXi7 set?

The stock sets are 4-iron through pitching wedge and 5-iron through pitching wedge, with the 3-iron and the 51-degree approach wedge sold individually. A 57-degree sand wedge is offered custom order and right hand only.

What is the difference between the Srixon ZXi7 and ZXi5?

The ZXi7 is the players model, a single-piece forged S15C body with a PureFrame ridge, a thin topline, minimal offset and traditional lofts (32-degree 7-iron). The ZXi5 is the players-distance model, with a softened forged face plate, MainFrame milling, a wider sole, more offset and stronger lofts (31-degree 7-iron).

Where it sits in the lineup

Introduced in 2024, the Srixon ZXi7 is a current players set in the Srixon range, with no replacement announced. It replaced the Srixon ZX7 Mk II. New sets are built to the standard lofts, lies and lengths in the chart above, though a fitting can move any of them — so confirm the numbers on the order sheet. Used sets appear on eBay, 2nd Swing and Golf Avenue, and should be measured against this chart before buying.
