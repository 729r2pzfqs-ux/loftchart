About the Srixon Z 565

The Z 565 is the cavity-back distance model in Srixon's 2016 three-model Z Series iron line, sitting below the Z 765 muscle cavity and the Z 965 blade. Srixon describes it as a 1020 carbon steel body that is precision forged, with a high-strength SUP10 face insert for ball speed, a revised Tour V.T. sole and grooves five percent larger than the previous Z 545 generation. Lofts are moderately strong for a forged cavity back, with a 31-degree 7-iron and a 44-degree pitching wedge, and they carry over unchanged into the Z 585 that replaced it. The stock range is 3-iron through pitching wedge in right and left hand, with a 50-degree approach wedge in right hand only. Srixon published this model's offset figures directly in inches, so no unit conversion was applied. The Tour V.T. sole is listed with separate leading- and trailing-edge bounce figures rather than a single bounce number, so bounce is omitted here. Swing weights below are the steel-shaft column; the graphite build is one point lighter throughout. Srixon announced the club in July 2016 and it reached retail on 16 September 2016, so it is often catalogued as a 2017 model.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Srixon Z 565 7-iron?

The Srixon Z 565 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a stock Srixon Z 565 set?

The stock range is 3-iron through pitching wedge, available in both right and left hand, plus a 50-degree approach wedge offered in right hand only.

What is the difference between the Srixon Z 565 and Z 765?

The Z 565 is the forged cavity-back distance model, with a SUP10 face insert and stronger lofts (31-degree 7-iron). The Z 765 is the muscle-cavity players model, forged from 1020 carbon steel throughout, with less offset and weaker lofts (32-degree 7-iron).

Did the lofts change between the Srixon Z 565 and the Z 585?

No. The Z 585 carries over the Z 565 lofts unchanged, from a 20-degree 3-iron through a 31-degree 7-iron to a 50-degree approach wedge.

Finding a used set

The Srixon Z 565 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
