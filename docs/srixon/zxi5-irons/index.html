About the Srixon ZXi5

The ZXi5 is the players-distance model in Srixon's ZXi iron line, which replaced the ZX Mk II generation. It is built with Srixon's i-Forged process: Condensed Forging, in which a second forging step presses raised features back into the head to add localised strength along the topline and high toe, which in turn allows softer steel and softer heat treatment elsewhere. Srixon states the ZXi5 face plate is 14 percent softer than the previous generation. Behind the face sits the third generation of MainFrame milling, and tungsten weighting sets the centre of gravity. Lofts carry over unchanged from the ZX5 Mk II, with a 31-degree 7-iron and a 44-degree pitching wedge. The published lengths look a quarter-inch longer than the ZX5 Mk II, but they are not: Srixon changed how it measures and publishes iron length to match USGA and R&A industry guidelines, and states directly that the actual length of the irons has not changed. The figures below are Srixon's published ZXi-convention lengths, so comparing them with the pre-ZXi models in this database will overstate the difference by about a quarter inch. Heads run 3-iron through a 56-degree sand wedge, with the 3-iron and one sand wedge variant custom-order only and the sand wedges right hand only. Offsets are converted from Srixon's published millimetre figures (the 7-iron is 3.2 mm, or 0.126 inches). Swing weights are the S-flex steel column. Srixon unveiled the line in late 2024 and it reached retail in January 2025, so it is commonly catalogued as a 2025 model.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Srixon ZXi5 7-iron?

The Srixon ZXi5 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a published steel-shaft length of 37.25 inches.

Why is the Srixon ZXi5 7-iron listed at 37.25 inches when the ZX5 Mk II was 37 inches?

Only the measuring convention changed. Srixon altered how it measures and publishes iron length to match USGA and R&A industry guidelines, which added about a quarter inch to the published figures, and states that the actual length of the irons has not changed.

What clubs come in a stock Srixon ZXi5 set?

Heads run 3-iron through a 56-degree sand wedge, with 4-iron through approach wedge the usual stock range in right and left hand. Srixon lists the 3-iron and one sand wedge variant as custom order only, and the sand wedges as right hand only.

Did the lofts change between the Srixon ZX5 Mk II and the ZXi5?

No. The ZXi5 carries over the ZX5 Mk II lofts unchanged, from a 20-degree 3-iron through a 31-degree 7-iron to a 50-degree approach wedge.

Finding a used set

The Srixon ZXi5 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
