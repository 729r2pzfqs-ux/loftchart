About the Srixon Z 585

The Z 585 is the distance half of Srixon's 2018 two-model Z Series iron line, sitting below the tour-oriented Z 785. It uses a body forged from soft 1020 carbon steel with a SUP10 face insert, and adds a Speed Groove — an internal channel behind the face insert that increases face flex for ball speed. Lofts are moderately strong for a compact head, with a 31-degree 7-iron and a 44-degree pitching wedge. The stock range is 3-iron through pitching wedge in right and left hand, with a 50-degree approach wedge in right hand only. Srixon published this model's offset figures directly in inches, so no unit conversion was applied. The sole is Srixon's Tour V.T. design, for which Srixon lists separate leading- and trailing-edge bounce figures rather than a single bounce number, so bounce is omitted here.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Srixon Z 585 7-iron?

The Srixon Z 585 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a stock Srixon Z 585 set?

The stock range is 3-iron through pitching wedge, available in both right and left hand, plus a 50-degree approach wedge offered in right hand only.

What is the difference between the Srixon Z 585 and Z 785?

The Z 585 is the distance model, with a SUP10 face insert and a Speed Groove for extra face flex and stronger lofts (31-degree 7-iron). The Z 785 is the players model, forged from a single billet of 1020 carbon steel with a tour cavity and weaker lofts (32-degree 7-iron).

Finding a used set

The Srixon Z 585 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
