About the Srixon ZX7 Mk II

The ZX7 Mk II is the players model in Srixon's second ZX iron generation, announced on 11 January 2023 and sitting above the ZX5 Mk II and ZX4 Mk II. Its defining feature is PureFrame, a ridge forged into the body just behind the sweet spot as an 80 percent thicker section of 1020 carbon steel, intended to reduce vibration at impact. Lofts carry over unchanged from the original ZX7, with a 32-degree 7-iron and a 46-degree pitching wedge, and it has the narrowest sole and least offset in the line at 15.1 mm and 2.8 mm at the 7-iron. Heads run 3-iron through a 51-degree approach wedge plus a 57-degree sand wedge, with 3-PW in right and left hand and the AW and SW in right hand only. Offsets below are converted from Srixon's published millimetre figures (for example the 7-iron is 2.8 mm, or 0.110 inches).

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Srixon ZX7 Mk II 7-iron?

The Srixon ZX7 Mk II 7-iron has a loft of 32 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a stock Srixon ZX7 Mk II set?

Heads run 3-iron through a 51-degree approach wedge, plus a 57-degree sand wedge. The 3-iron through pitching wedge are offered in both right and left hand; the approach and sand wedges are right hand only.

What is PureFrame on the Srixon ZX7 Mk II?

PureFrame is a ridge forged into the back of the head directly behind the sweet spot, made as an 80 percent thicker section of 1020 carbon steel to cut unwanted vibration at impact. It was exclusive to the ZX7 Mk II within the ZX Mk II line.

Finding a used set

The Srixon ZX7 Mk II is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
