About the Srixon ZX4 Mk II

The ZX4 Mk II is the most forgiving model in Srixon's second ZX iron generation, announced on 11 January 2023 alongside the ZX5 Mk II and ZX7 Mk II. It keeps the hollow head design of the original ZX4 and carries the second generation of Srixon's MainFrame technology, a variable-thickness pattern of grooves, channels and cavities milled into the back of the face that boosts COR and moves mass into the toe and sole. Lofts are the strongest in the ZX Mk II line and are unchanged from the original ZX4, with a 28.5-degree 7-iron and a 43-degree pitching wedge — 2.5 degrees stronger than the ZX5 Mk II at the 7-iron. Srixon's own comparison chart gives the ZX4 Mk II the longest blade length in the line (80.9 mm at the 7-iron) and the most hosel offset (3.4 mm), with a 20.7 mm sole, but the same 6.0 mm topline as the ZX5 and ZX7. Heads run 4-iron through a 56-degree sand wedge, with 4-PW in right and left hand and the approach and sand wedges right hand only. Offsets below are converted from Srixon's published millimetre figures (for example the 7-iron is 3.4 mm, or 0.134 inches). Swing weights are the steel-shaft column; the graphite build is roughly three points lighter. Srixon does not publish a single bounce figure for the Tour V.T. sole, so bounce is omitted.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Srixon ZX4 Mk II 7-iron?

The Srixon ZX4 Mk II 7-iron has a loft of 28.5 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a stock Srixon ZX4 Mk II set?

Heads run 4-iron through a 56-degree sand wedge. The 4-iron through pitching wedge are offered in both right and left hand; the 49-degree approach wedge and the sand wedge are right hand only.

How much stronger are the Srixon ZX4 Mk II lofts than the ZX5 Mk II?

By 2.5 degrees at the 7-iron — 28.5 degrees against 31 degrees — and by 1 degree at the pitching wedge, 43 degrees against 44 degrees.

Did the lofts change between the Srixon ZX4 and the ZX4 Mk II?

No. The ZX4 Mk II carries over the ZX4 lofts unchanged, from a 21-degree 4-iron through a 28.5-degree 7-iron to a 49-degree approach wedge. The Mk II adds a 56-degree sand wedge to the range.

Finding a used set

The Srixon ZX4 Mk II is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
