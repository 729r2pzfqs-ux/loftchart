About the Nike VR Pro Combo

The VR Pro Combo is Nike's forged combo set, built around three head shapes that change through the bag: a pocket-cavity design in the 3- to 5-irons for launch, a split-cavity mid-iron, and a full muscle-back blade from the 8-iron down. Lofts are traditional throughout, with a 35-degree 7-iron and a 47-degree pitching wedge — far weaker than the distance irons of the same era. The stock set is 3-iron through pitching wedge, and the sole carries progressive bounce that grows from 2 degrees in the 3-iron to 9 degrees in the wedge. It was the iron Tiger Woods and Rory McIlroy played versions of during Nike's tour-staff era. Offset is not published for this set and is omitted below rather than inferred.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Nike VR Pro Combo 7-iron?

The Nike VR Pro Combo 7-iron has a loft of 35 degrees, with a 62.5-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Nike VR Pro Combo?

The VR Pro Combo pitching wedge is 47 degrees. That is a traditional loft, around 3 to 5 degrees weaker than a game-improvement pitching wedge of the same period.

Are the Nike VR Pro Combo irons blades?

Only partly. It is a combo set: the 3- through 5-irons use a pocket cavity, the 6- and 7-irons a split cavity, and the 8-iron through pitching wedge are full muscle-back blades.

Finding a used set

The Nike VR Pro Combo ran from 2011 to 2014 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
