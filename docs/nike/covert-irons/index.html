About the Nike VR_S Covert

The VR_S Covert irons were the iron half of Nike's 2013 Covert launch, sold alongside the cavity-back Covert driver. They use a deep undercut cavity with a thin, high-strength face and a modified sole, and carry noticeably more offset than Nike's players irons — 0.150 inches at the 7-iron, tapering to 0.040 inches in the gap wedge. Lofts are moderately strong for the period, with a 31-degree 7-iron and a 44-degree pitching wedge. The stock set listed here is 4-iron through gap wedge; a 3-iron and a sand wedge were available as add-ons. Bounce and swing weight were not published in the source table and are omitted rather than inferred.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Nike VR_S Covert 7-iron?

The Nike VR_S Covert 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37.5-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Nike VR_S Covert?

The VR_S Covert pitching wedge is 44 degrees, with a 48-degree gap wedge in the stock set to fill the gap below it.

How much offset do the Nike VR_S Covert irons have?

The 7-iron has 0.150 inches of offset. Offset reduces progressively through the set, from 0.195 inches in the 4-iron down to 0.040 inches in the gap wedge.

Finding a used set

The Nike VR_S Covert is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
