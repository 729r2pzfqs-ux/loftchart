About the Nike Vapor Pro

The Vapor Pro is the most compact iron in Nike's 2015 Vapor family, sitting below the Vapor Pro Combo, Vapor Speed and Vapor Fly. It is a forged players iron with a thin topline, a narrow sole and minimal offset — 0.080 inches at the 7-iron — built around a modified muscle-back profile rather than a deep cavity. Lofts are fully traditional, with a 35-degree 7-iron and a 47-degree pitching wedge, unchanged from the VR Pro Combo it replaced. The stock set is 3-iron through pitching wedge with progressive bounce running from 3 degrees to 10 degrees, built to D3.5 swing weight with Dynamic Gold shafts.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Nike Vapor Pro 7-iron?

The Nike Vapor Pro 7-iron has a loft of 35 degrees, with a 62.5-degree lie and a 37.25-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Nike Vapor Pro?

The Vapor Pro pitching wedge is 47 degrees, a traditional loft that leaves a clean gap to a 51 or 52-degree gap wedge.

How do the Nike Vapor Pro lofts compare to the VR Pro Combo?

They are identical through the set, from a 21-degree 3-iron to a 47-degree pitching wedge. The Vapor Pro is a quarter-inch longer in the mid and long irons and publishes offset figures, which the VR Pro Combo did not.

Finding a used set

The Nike Vapor Pro is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
