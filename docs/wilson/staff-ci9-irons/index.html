About the Wilson Staff Ci9

The Staff Ci9 was Wilson Staff's mid-handicap cavity-back iron for 2009, sold alongside the Fi5 and the FG Tour blades and aimed at what Wilson called the "control player". It uses a cast stainless steel head with a deep undercut cavity and progressive head sizing, getting more compact through the short irons. Lofts are moderately strong for the period, with a 32-degree 7-iron and a 44-degree pitching wedge. Clubs were offered from 3-iron through sand wedge, with 4-PW the common stock set and gap and sand wedges sold as matching add-ons. Lengths run half an inch apart from a 37.5-inch 7-iron. All figures are the standard men's steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Wilson Staff Ci9 7-iron?

The Wilson Staff Ci9 7-iron has a loft of 32 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37.5-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Wilson Staff Ci9?

The Ci9 pitching wedge is 44 degrees. A matching 48-degree gap wedge and 56-degree sand wedge were also offered.

What clubs came in a Wilson Staff Ci9 set?

The Ci9 was built from 3-iron through sand wedge. The usual stock set was 4-iron through pitching wedge, with the gap and sand wedges available separately.

Finding a used set

The Wilson Staff Ci9 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
