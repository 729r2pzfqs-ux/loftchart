About the Wilson DYNAPWR

DYNAPWR is the 2025 replacement for the cast Dynapower and is a different head, not a restyling of it. Wilson dropped the Power Hole slots that had run through several generations of its distance irons and moved to a hollow body with an Energy Transfer Chassis, a PKR-Cup cup face and a Velocity Optimization Channel. Lofts are half a degree to a degree weaker than the 2023 Dynapower through most of the set, with a 28-degree 7-iron against the Dynapower's 27 and a 42.5-degree pitching wedge against 42, which is unusual for a successor and reflects the higher launch the cup face produces. The set runs 4-iron through a 48-degree gap wedge and was announced on 29 January 2025 alongside a super-game-improvement DYNAPWR Max with weaker lofts again. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Wilson DYNAPWR 7-iron?

The Wilson DYNAPWR 7-iron has a loft of 28 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37.5-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a Wilson DYNAPWR set?

The set runs 4-iron through pitching wedge plus a 48-degree gap wedge, sold as a six or seven piece set depending on the retailer.

Is the DYNAPWR the same iron as the 2023 Dynapower?

No. The 2025 DYNAPWR is a different head. It replaced the cast Dynapower's Power Hole sole slots with a hollow body, an Energy Transfer Chassis and a PKR-Cup face, and its lofts are half a degree to a degree weaker through the set.

How do DYNAPWR lofts compare with Dynapower lofts?

DYNAPWR is slightly weaker. The 7-iron is 28 degrees against the Dynapower's 27, and the pitching wedge is 42.5 degrees against 42. Lengths and lies are unchanged.

Finding a used set

The Wilson DYNAPWR is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
