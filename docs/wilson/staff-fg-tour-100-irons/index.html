About the Wilson Staff FG Tour 100

The FG Tour 100 is the commemorative blade Wilson showed at the PGA Merchandise Show in early 2014 to mark 100 years since the company was incorporated, and it reached shops in mid-February that year. The head is a three-piece forging with a milled diamond-pattern face, a medallion badge on the muscle, triple-knurled hosel engraving and a Fluid Feel bore-through insert, styled after Wilson's 1971 Button Back Dynapower. Lofts are traditional, running from a 21-degree 3-iron to a 47-degree pitching wedge with a 35-degree 7-iron, and offset is very small at 0.110 inches in the 3-iron tapering to 0.050 inches at the pitching wedge. The stock set is 3-iron through pitching wedge with a 51-degree gap wedge available to match. The year_discontinued above follows this project's convention of recording the year the successor was announced; in practice the FG Tour 100 was a short commemorative run and Wilson had no direct blade replacement until the 2019 Staff Model Blade. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Wilson Staff FG Tour 100 7-iron?

The Wilson Staff FG Tour 100 7-iron has a loft of 35 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37.25-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a Wilson Staff FG Tour 100 set?

The stock set is 3-iron through pitching wedge, with a 51-degree gap wedge available to match.

Why is it called the FG Tour 100?

It was built for Wilson's centenary in 2014, one hundred years after the company was incorporated, and its styling borrows from the 1971 Button Back Dynapower blade.

How do the FG Tour 100 lofts compare with the Staff Model Blade?

The FG Tour 100 is a degree weaker in the short irons. Its 7-iron is 35 degrees against the Staff Model Blade's 34, and its pitching wedge is 47 degrees against 46.

Finding a used set

The Wilson Staff FG Tour 100 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
