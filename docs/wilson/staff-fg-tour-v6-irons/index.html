About the Wilson Staff FG Tour V6

The FG Tour V6 was Wilson's tour cavity back, announced in the autumn of 2016 and sold as a 2017 model. Heads are forged from soft 8620 carbon steel and carry 20 grams of tungsten, up from 18 grams in the FG Tour V4, split 12 grams toe and 8 grams heel in the 3 to 5 irons, moved to the centre of the sole in the 6 and 7 irons and dropped entirely in the scoring clubs. The profile is a players' one, with a thin topline, short blade length and minimal offset, and lofts are traditional at 35 degrees for the 7-iron and 47 degrees for the pitching wedge. Clubs run 3-iron through pitching wedge with a 51-degree gap wedge available, sold most often as a 3-PW or 4-PW set. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Wilson Staff FG Tour V6 7-iron?

The Wilson Staff FG Tour V6 7-iron has a loft of 35 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37.25-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a Wilson Staff FG Tour V6 set?

Clubs run 3-iron through pitching wedge with a 51-degree gap wedge available. Sets were normally sold as 3-PW or 4-PW.

How much tungsten is in the FG Tour V6?

Twenty grams, up from eighteen in the FG Tour V4. It is split 12 grams toe and 8 grams heel in the 3 to 5 irons, centred in the sole in the 6 and 7 irons, and absent from the 8-iron down.

How do the FG Tour V6 lofts compare with the Staff Model CB that replaced it?

The V6 is a degree weaker in the short irons. Its 7-iron is 35 degrees against the Staff Model CB's 34, and its pitching wedge is 47 degrees against 46.

Finding a used set

The Wilson Staff FG Tour V6 ran from 2016 to 2020 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
