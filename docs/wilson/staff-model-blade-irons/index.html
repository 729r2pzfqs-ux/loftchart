About the Wilson Staff Model Blade

The Staff Model Blade is Wilson's muscle-back tour iron, unveiled in January 2019 after prototype versions were played on tour and carried forward through later refreshes alongside the Staff Model CB cavity back. Heads are soft-forged from 8620 carbon steel and then precision milled, with a compact profile, minimal offset and Wilson's Fluid Feel Hosel, which removes mass from the hosel to allow a slightly larger head and wider sole. Lofts are traditional, with a 34-degree 7-iron and a 46-degree pitching wedge, matching the Staff Model CB through the set. Clubs are built from 2-iron through pitching wedge, with 3-PW and 4-PW the usual stock sets. Figures below are the standard men's steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Wilson Staff Model Blade 7-iron?

The Wilson Staff Model Blade 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37.25-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a Wilson Staff Model Blade set?

The Blade is built from 2-iron through pitching wedge, and is normally sold as a 3-PW or 4-PW set. There is no gap wedge in the blade range.

Do the Staff Model Blade and Staff Model CB share lofts?

Yes. Both run 18 degrees at the 2-iron to 46 degrees at the pitching wedge with the same lengths, so the two can be combined in a mixed set. Only the CB adds a 50-degree gap wedge.

Finding a used set

The Wilson Staff Model Blade is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
