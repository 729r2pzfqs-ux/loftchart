About the Wilson Staff Model CB

The Staff Model CB is Wilson's forged players cavity back, announced in October 2020 as the successor to the FG Tour V6 and still in the line through later refreshes. The head is soft-forged 8620 carbon steel with a Tri-Brace stabiliser bracing the cavity in three places and about twenty grams of high-density tungsten in the toe of the long and mid irons. It is a compact players shape with a thin topline and very little offset, tapering from 0.12 inches at the 2-iron to 0.04 inches at the gap wedge. Lofts are traditional for a players iron, with a 34-degree 7-iron and a 46-degree pitching wedge. Clubs are built from 2-iron through gap wedge, with 4-PW the stock set.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Wilson Staff Model CB 7-iron?

The Wilson Staff Model CB 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37.25-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a stock Wilson Staff Model CB set?

The stock set is 4-iron through pitching wedge. The model is built from 2-iron through a 50-degree gap wedge, so longer irons and the gap wedge can be added.

How is the Staff Model CB different from the Staff Model Blade?

The CB is a forged cavity back with a Tri-Brace stabiliser and tungsten toe weighting for more forgiveness, while the Blade is a muscle-back. They share the same lofts, lies and lengths from the 2-iron through the pitching wedge.

Finding a used set

The Wilson Staff Model CB is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
