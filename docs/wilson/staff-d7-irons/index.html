About the Wilson Staff D7

The D7 was Wilson's distance-oriented game-improvement iron for the 2019 season, replacing the D300 and using a cast stainless steel head with Wilson's Power Hole slots around the perimeter of the sole and toe. The number of Power Hole rows is reduced through the set, from three rows in the long irons down to one in the pitching wedge, trading speed in the long irons for feel in the short ones. Lofts are strong, with a 28-degree 7-iron and a 43-degree pitching wedge, roughly a degree stronger than the D300 through the 4 to 8 irons. The stock steel set is 5-iron through pitching wedge with a 48-degree gap wedge available to match. Figures below are the standard men's steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Wilson Staff D7 7-iron?

The Wilson Staff D7 7-iron has a loft of 28 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37.5-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a stock Wilson Staff D7 set?

The stock steel set is 5-iron through pitching wedge, with a 48-degree gap wedge available as a matching add-on.

How strong are the Wilson Staff D7 lofts?

Strong for a game-improvement iron of its era. The 7-iron is 28 degrees and the pitching wedge is 43 degrees, about a degree stronger than the D300 it replaced.

Finding a used set

The Wilson Staff D7 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
