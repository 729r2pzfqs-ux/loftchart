About the Wilson Staff D9

The D9 replaced the D7 as Wilson's distance game-improvement iron for 2021, keeping the cast stainless steel construction and the perimeter Power Hole slots but pairing them with a thinner, faster face and a lower centre of gravity. Lofts are very strong: the 7-iron is 27 degrees and the pitching wedge 42 degrees, a degree stronger through the mid irons than the D7. Offset is substantial in the long irons at just over a quarter of an inch on the 4-iron, tapering to 0.136 inches at the pitching wedge. The set runs 4-iron through sand wedge, with 5-PW plus a 47-degree gap wedge the usual stock make-up. All figures are the standard men's steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Wilson Staff D9 7-iron?

The Wilson Staff D9 7-iron has a loft of 27 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37.5-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a stock Wilson Staff D9 set?

The D9 was built from 4-iron through sand wedge. The common stock set is 5-iron through pitching wedge plus a 47-degree gap wedge.

How do Wilson Staff D9 lofts compare to the D7?

The D9 is about a degree stronger through the mid irons: a 27-degree 7-iron versus 28 degrees, and a 42-degree pitching wedge versus 43 degrees.

Finding a used set

The Wilson Staff D9 ran from 2021 to 2023 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
