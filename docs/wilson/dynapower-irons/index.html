About the Wilson Dynapower

Dynapower revived a historic Wilson name for 2023, replacing the D9 as the brand's distance game-improvement iron. The cast stainless steel head moves mass to the toe for a higher moment of inertia and keeps the centre of gravity low for a high launch and steep descent. Lofts and lengths carry over from the D9 unchanged: a 27-degree 7-iron at 37.5 inches and a 42-degree pitching wedge, with offset tapering from 0.236 inches at the 5-iron to 0.136 inches at the pitching wedge. The stock set is 5-iron through pitching wedge plus a 47-degree gap wedge, with a 4-iron and a 53-degree sand wedge available to match. A separate forged Dynapower model with weaker lofts was sold alongside it and is a different head.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Wilson Dynapower 7-iron?

The Wilson Dynapower 7-iron has a loft of 27 degrees, with a 62-degree lie and a 37.5-inch steel-shaft length.

What clubs come in a stock Wilson Dynapower set?

The stock set is 5-iron through pitching wedge plus a 47-degree gap wedge. A 4-iron and a 53-degree sand wedge are available to match.

Are the Wilson Dynapower lofts the same as the D9?

Yes. The cast Dynapower carries the D9 ladder unchanged, from an 18-degree 4-iron to a 42-degree pitching wedge, with the same lengths, lies and offsets.

Is the Dynapower Forged the same iron?

No. The forged Dynapower is a separate players-distance head with weaker lofts, including a 30.5-degree 7-iron and a 44-degree pitching wedge.

Finding a used set

The Wilson Dynapower ran from 2023 to 2025 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
