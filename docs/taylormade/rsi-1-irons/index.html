About the TaylorMade RSi 1

The RSi 1 was TaylorMade's mainstream game-improvement iron for 2015 and the model that introduced Face Slot Technology, a pair of slots near the heel and toe of the face in the 3- through 8-irons intended to keep ball speed up on off-centre strikes. The 3- through 7-irons also carry the ThruSlot Speed Pocket in the sole, and the head uses a deep undercut cavity with Inverted Cone Technology. Lofts are strong, with a 30.5-degree 7-iron and a 44-degree pitching wedge, and the stock range runs 3-iron through a 60-degree lob wedge. Specs below are the men's standard steel-shaft build with the True Temper Reax 90 shaft.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade RSi 1 7-iron?

The TaylorMade RSi 1 7-iron has a loft of 30.5 degrees, with a 63-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

What is Face Slot Technology on the RSi irons?

Face Slots are two slots cut near the heel and toe of the clubface in the 3- through 8-irons. They let the face flex more uniformly across its width, which protects ball speed on strikes toward the heel and toe. The 3- through 7-irons additionally have the ThruSlot Speed Pocket in the sole for low-face strikes.

What is the difference between the RSi 1 and the RSi 2?

The RSi 1 is the larger game-improvement model with stronger lofts; its 7-iron is 30.5 degrees against 31 degrees for the RSi 2, and its pitching wedge is 44 degrees against 45. The RSi 2 is a more compact multi-material head with a forged face in the short irons and tungsten weighting in the long irons.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade RSi 1 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
