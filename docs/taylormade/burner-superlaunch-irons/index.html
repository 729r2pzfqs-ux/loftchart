About the TaylorMade Burner SuperLaunch

The Burner SuperLaunch was TaylorMade's maximum-forgiveness iron of 2010-2011, sold alongside the Burner 2.0 rather than replacing it and marketed as the company's highest-MOI iron to that point. It uses an ultra-large deep cavity, a low-profile long-iron head, an extra-wide multi-functional sole and Inverted Cone Technology, and it was one of the first TaylorMade irons built with the 2010 conforming groove design. Lofts sit a degree weaker than the concurrent Burner 2.0, with a 33-degree 7-iron and a 46-degree pitching wedge, and the set tops out at a sand wedge. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade Burner SuperLaunch 7-iron?

The TaylorMade Burner SuperLaunch 7-iron has a loft of 33 degrees, with a 63-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

How do the Burner SuperLaunch lofts compare to the Burner 2.0?

The SuperLaunch is 2 degrees weaker at the 7-iron (33 degrees versus 31) and 1 degree weaker at the pitching wedge (46 versus 45). The SuperLaunch was built for higher launch and easier carry rather than for maximum distance.

Is there a Burner SuperFast iron set?

No. SuperFast was a TaylorMade driver, fairway wood and rescue name. The SuperFast branding appears on this iron only as the shaft designation, the Burner SuperFast 85 steel shaft fitted to the Burner SuperLaunch irons.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade Burner SuperLaunch is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
