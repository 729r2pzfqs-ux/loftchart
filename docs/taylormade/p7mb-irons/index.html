About the TaylorMade P7MB

The P7MB is TaylorMade's muscleback blade, forged from 1025 carbon steel using a compact grain forging process and finished with milled faces and grooves. It carries the least offset of any club in the P-Series, from 1.9mm in the 3-iron down to 1.4mm in the pitching wedge, with a thin topline and a narrow sole. The 7-iron is 35 degrees and the pitching wedge 47 degrees, one degree weaker than the P7MC cavity back through the 9-iron, and the set is offered 3-iron through pitching wedge with no stock gap wedge. Specs below are the men's standard right-handed steel build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade P7MB 7-iron?

The TaylorMade P7MB 7-iron has a loft of 35 degrees, with a 62.5-degree lie and a 37.00-inch steel-shaft length.

Is the P7MB a true blade?

Yes. The P7MB is a one-piece 1025 carbon steel muscleback with no cavity, no tungsten weighting and the smallest offset in the P-Series, ranging from 1.9mm in the 3-iron to 1.4mm in the pitching wedge.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the P7MB?

The P7MB pitching wedge is 47 degrees, the same as the P7MC. The set runs 3-iron through pitching wedge with no stock approach or gap wedge.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade P7MB is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
