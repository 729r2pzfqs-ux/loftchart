About the TaylorMade Burner Plus

The Burner Plus was the higher-launching, more forgiving companion to the 2009 Burner iron, aimed at players who wanted the same Burner distance with a larger head and a wider sole. It combines a thin high-COR face with Inverted Cone Technology, advanced perimeter weighting and TaylorMade's SuperFast light-shaft and light-grip package. Lofts run strong for the period, with a 32-degree 7-iron and a 45-degree pitching wedge, and the stock set extends to a 60-degree lob wedge. Specs below are the men's standard steel-shaft build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade Burner Plus 7-iron?

The TaylorMade Burner Plus 7-iron has a loft of 32 degrees, with a 63-degree lie and a 37.25-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the difference between the Burner Plus and the Burner 2.0?

The Burner Plus is the 2009 model and the Burner 2.0 replaced it in 2010. The Burner 2.0 is 1 degree stronger through the mid-irons (31-degree 7-iron versus 32 degrees) and uses slightly flatter lies and a different length progression, though both sets share a 45-degree pitching wedge.

How many clubs were in the Burner Plus iron set?

The stock Burner Plus range ran from a 19-degree 3-iron to a 60-degree lob wedge, including a 50-degree approach wedge and a 55-degree sand wedge. Burner Rescue clubs were also offered as combo-set replacements for the long irons.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade Burner Plus ran from 2009 to 2010 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
