About the TaylorMade M1

The M1 iron was TaylorMade's only M-family players iron, sold for a single season in 2017 alongside the larger M2. It pairs a thin high-COR face with Face Slot Technology in the heel and toe and a Speed Pocket in the sole, while a 180-degree fluted hosel and high-density tungsten in the 3-iron through 7-iron move the centre of gravity low. Head size, blade length and offset are smaller than the M2, and offset runs from 4.0 mm in the 3-iron to 1.6 mm in the sand wedge. The 7-iron is 30.5 degrees, about two degrees weaker than the M2 of the same year. Specs below are the men's standard steel-shaft build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade M1 7-iron?

The TaylorMade M1 7-iron has a loft of 30.5 degrees, with a 62.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

How do the M1 irons differ from the M2 irons?

The M1 is the more compact of the two. It has a smaller head, a thinner topline, a shorter blade length and less offset, and its lofts are about two degrees weaker through the set: 30.5 degrees at the 7-iron against 28.5 degrees for the 2016 M2.

Do the TaylorMade M1 irons use tungsten?

Yes. High-density tungsten is placed in the 3-iron through 7-iron heads to lower the centre of gravity and allow a narrower sole. The short irons do not carry the tungsten weight.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the M1 irons?

The M1 pitching wedge is 45 degrees. The set also offers a 50-degree approach wedge and a 55-degree sand wedge, and runs from a 3-iron at 18 degrees.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade M1 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
