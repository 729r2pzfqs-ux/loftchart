About the TaylorMade P770

The 2020 P770 is a compact hollow-body forged players iron that sits between the blade-like P7MC and the larger P790 in TaylorMade's P-Series. It uses the same SpeedFoam-filled hollow construction and forged face as the P790 but in a smaller head with a thinner topline, less offset and a narrower sole. Lofts sit roughly two to three degrees weaker than the P790 through the middle of the set, with a 33-degree 7-iron and a 46-degree pitching wedge, and the published range runs 3-iron through approach wedge. Specs below are the men's standard right-handed steel build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade P770 7-iron?

The TaylorMade P770 7-iron has a loft of 33 degrees, with a 62.5-degree lie and a 37.00-inch steel-shaft length.

How do P770 lofts compare with the P790?

The P770 is 2.5 degrees weaker than the P790 through most of the set: a 33-degree 7-iron against 30.5 degrees, and a 46-degree pitching wedge against 45. Lengths and lie angles are the same across the two models.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the P770?

The P770 pitching wedge is 46 degrees, with a 51-degree approach wedge available to complete the 3-iron through approach wedge range.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade P770 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
