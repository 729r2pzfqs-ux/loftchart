About the TaylorMade Burner

The Burner iron was TaylorMade's mainstream distance iron of the SuperFast era, built around the idea of eight individually engineered heads rather than one scaled blueprint. Long irons are low-profile and forgiving, mid-irons are tuned for distance control and short irons for response, with a thin face, Inverted Cone Technology and a multi-functional sole running through the set. The SuperFast package uses a light 85-gram steel shaft and a 47-gram grip. Lofts are strong for the period, with a 31-degree 7-iron and a 45-degree pitching wedge. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade Burner 7-iron?

The TaylorMade Burner 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, with a 63-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the difference between the Burner and the Burner Plus?

The Burner is the smaller, more workable of the pair, with a 31-degree 7-iron and flatter lies. The Burner Plus uses a larger head and wider sole for higher launch and more forgiveness, and its lofts are a degree weaker through the mid-irons at 32 degrees in the 7-iron.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the TaylorMade Burner?

The Burner pitching wedge is 45 degrees, with a 50-degree approach wedge, 55-degree sand wedge and 60-degree lob wedge available in the set.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade Burner ran from 2009 to 2010 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
