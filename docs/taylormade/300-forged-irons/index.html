About the TaylorMade 300 Forged

The 300 Forged was TaylorMade's tour iron for 2001, a compact carbon-steel head double-struck in a forging process the company called coining, with a thin topline and only a slight amount of offset. The centre of gravity moves progressively through the set and an impact pad sits behind the sweet spot for feel. Lofts are traditional rather than strong, with a 35-degree 7-iron and a 48-degree pitching wedge, and the documented range runs from a 1-iron to a pitching wedge. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade 300 Forged 7-iron?

The TaylorMade 300 Forged 7-iron has a loft of 35 degrees, with a 61.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

What year did the TaylorMade 300 Forged irons come out?

The 300 Forged irons were introduced in 2001 and were sold until the rac line took over the TaylorMade iron range in 2003.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the TaylorMade 300 Forged?

The 300 Forged pitching wedge is 48 degrees. The set uses traditional loft gaps of 3 to 4 degrees, so it runs roughly 4 to 6 degrees weaker than a modern game-improvement set.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade 300 Forged is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
