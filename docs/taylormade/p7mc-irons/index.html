About the TaylorMade P7MC

The P7MC is TaylorMade's tour cavity back, forged from 1025 carbon steel using a compact grain forging process and finished with milled faces and grooves. It shares its head shape and length progression with the P7MB muscleback but adds a small perimeter-weighted cavity and about 1mm more offset per club for a slight gain in forgiveness. Lofts are one degree stronger than the P7MB through the 9-iron, with a 34-degree 7-iron and a 47-degree pitching wedge, and the set runs 3-iron through pitching wedge with no stock gap wedge. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade P7MC 7-iron?

The TaylorMade P7MC 7-iron has a loft of 34 degrees, with a 62.5-degree lie and a 37.00-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the difference between the P7MC and the P7MB?

The P7MC is a small cavity back and the P7MB is a full muscleback. They share lie angles and lengths, but the P7MC is one degree stronger from 3-iron through 9-iron (34 degrees at the 7-iron against 35) and carries about 0.5 to 1mm more offset per club.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the P7MC?

The P7MC pitching wedge is 47 degrees. The set is offered 3-iron through pitching wedge, with no stock approach or gap wedge.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade P7MC is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
