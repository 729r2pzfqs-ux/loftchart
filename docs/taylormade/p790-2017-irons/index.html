About the TaylorMade P790 (2017)

The 2017 P790 was the first generation of TaylorMade's hollow-body forged distance iron, built around a 1.75mm wrapped forged 4140 face, an injected SpeedFoam filling and a high-density tungsten weight in the toe. It sat between the P770 players iron and the game-improvement M-series in TaylorMade's 2017 line, using stronger lofts than a traditional players iron: the 7-iron is 30.5 degrees and the pitching wedge 45 degrees. The first-generation set is shorter than the 2021 model, with a 36.75-inch 7-iron and 38.75-inch 3-iron, and it carries slightly more offset through the long irons. All numbers below are the men's standard right-handed steel build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade P790 (2017) 7-iron?

The TaylorMade P790 (2017) 7-iron has a loft of 30.5 degrees, with a 62.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

How do the 2017 P790 specs differ from the 2021 P790?

The lofts are identical across the two generations, including the 30.5-degree 7-iron and 45-degree pitching wedge. What changed is length and offset: the 2021 set is 0.25 inches longer in the 3-iron through 9-iron (37.00-inch 7-iron versus 36.75 inches) and drops long-iron offset from 3.9mm to 3.5mm in the 3-iron.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the 2017 P790?

The 2017 P790 pitching wedge is 45 degrees, and the matching approach wedge is 50 degrees. The published set runs 3-iron through approach wedge.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade P790 (2017) is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
