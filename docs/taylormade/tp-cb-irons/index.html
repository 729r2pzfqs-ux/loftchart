About the TaylorMade TP CB

The Tour Preferred CB was the most forgiving of TaylorMade's three 2014 Tour Preferred irons, sitting above the MC and MB in the range. It pairs a forged face with a cast undercut cavity and adds a Speed Pocket to the 3- through 7-irons, which let TaylorMade strengthen the lofts while keeping launch high. The head shape is progressive, with compact minimal-offset short irons and larger long irons carrying more offset, and the set runs from a 3-iron to a 51-degree approach wedge. The 7-iron is 32.5 degrees. Specs below are the men's standard steel-shaft build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade TP CB 7-iron?

The TaylorMade TP CB 7-iron has a loft of 32.5 degrees, with a 62.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

How do the TP CB lofts compare to the TP MB?

The TP CB is stronger through most of the set. Its 7-iron is 32.5 degrees against 35 degrees for the TP MB, and its pitching wedge is 46 degrees against 47. TaylorMade strengthened the CB lofts because the Speed Pocket in the 3- through 7-irons raises launch angle.

Does the TaylorMade TP CB have a Speed Pocket?

Yes, in the 3- through 7-irons. The slot in the sole increases ball speed on strikes low on the face, which is what allowed the stronger loft progression compared with the MB and MC models in the same 2014 Tour Preferred range.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade TP CB is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
