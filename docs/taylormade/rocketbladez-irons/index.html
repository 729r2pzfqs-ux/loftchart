About the TaylorMade RocketBladez

The RocketBladez was the first TaylorMade iron to carry the Speed Pocket, a slot cut into the sole behind the face of the long and mid irons that lets the lower face flex more and raises ball speed on shots struck low on the face. It was announced in late 2012 and sold as the 2013 game-improvement model, alongside a more compact RocketBladez Tour version with weaker lofts. The standard set is strongly lofted, with a 30.5-degree 7-iron. Specs below are the men's standard steel-shaft build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade RocketBladez 7-iron?

The standard TaylorMade RocketBladez 7-iron has a loft of 30.5 degrees, with a 63-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the Speed Pocket on the RocketBladez irons?

The Speed Pocket is a slot cut into the sole just behind the face on the long and mid irons. It lets the lower part of the face flex more at impact, which raises ball speed and launch on shots struck low on the face, where game-improvement players most often miss.

How do RocketBladez lofts compare to RocketBladez Tour?

The standard RocketBladez is considerably stronger. Its 7-iron is 30.5 degrees against roughly 33.75 degrees for the RocketBladez Tour, and its pitching wedge is 45 degrees against about 47 degrees.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade RocketBladez is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
