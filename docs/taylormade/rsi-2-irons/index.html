About the TaylorMade RSi 2

The RSi 2 sat above the RSi 1 in TaylorMade's 2015 iron range, offering the same Face Slot and ThruSlot Speed Pocket technologies in a more compact multi-material head. The long irons are cast with tungsten weighting low in the toe to lower the centre of gravity, while the short irons use a forged carbon steel face with milled grooves for feel and control. Lofts are about half a degree to a degree weaker than the RSi 1, with a 31-degree 7-iron and a 45-degree pitching wedge, and the published range runs from a 2-iron to a 55-degree sand wedge. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade RSi 2 7-iron?

The TaylorMade RSi 2 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, with a 62.5-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

Are the TaylorMade RSi 2 irons forged?

Partly. The RSi 2 uses progressive multi-material construction: the long irons are cast with tungsten weighting low in the toe, while the short irons have a forged carbon steel face with milled grooves. It is not a fully forged iron.

How do the RSi 2 lofts compare to the RSi 1?

The RSi 2 is marginally weaker. Its 7-iron is 31 degrees against 30.5 for the RSi 1 and its pitching wedge is 45 degrees against 44. The RSi 2 also uses a shorter length ladder, with a 36.75-inch 7-iron versus 37 inches.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade RSi 2 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
