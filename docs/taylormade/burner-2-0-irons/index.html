About the TaylorMade Burner 2.0

The Burner 2.0 was TaylorMade's mainstream game-improvement iron for 2010-2011, replacing the 2009 Burner. Each head was individually engineered rather than built from a single blueprint: thin, high-COR faces with Inverted Cone Technology in the long irons for ball speed, and progressively higher-CG short irons for control. TaylorMade positioned it as a 0-30 handicap iron. Lofts are notably strong for the era, with a 31-degree 7-iron and a 45-degree pitching wedge. Specs below are the men's standard steel-shaft build.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade Burner 2.0 7-iron?

The TaylorMade Burner 2.0 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, with a 63-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

Are TaylorMade Burner 2.0 irons strong-lofted?

Yes. The 2010 Burner 2.0 set runs about 3 to 4 degrees stronger than a traditional iron set, with a 31-degree 7-iron and a 45-degree pitching wedge, which is why they carry a full club further than older irons of the same number.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Burner 2.0?

The Burner 2.0 pitching wedge is 45 degrees. The matching approach wedge is 50 degrees, sand wedge 55 degrees and lob wedge 60 degrees.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade Burner 2.0 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
