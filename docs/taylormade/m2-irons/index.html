About the TaylorMade M2

The 2016 M2 was TaylorMade's distance-oriented game-improvement iron. Three weight-saving features, an L-shaped toe, a 360-degree undercut cavity and a thick-thin fluted hosel, let the designers push the centre of gravity very low, which is what allowed them to use unusually strong lofts without losing peak height. A large unsupported thin face with Inverted Cone Technology and a Speed Pocket in the sole add ball speed. The 7-iron is 28.5 degrees, roughly a traditional 5-iron loft. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade M2 7-iron?

The 2016 TaylorMade M2 7-iron has a loft of 28.5 degrees, with a 63-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length.

Why are the 2016 M2 iron lofts so strong?

TaylorMade used an L-shaped toe, a 360-degree undercut cavity and a thick-thin fluted hosel to move the centre of gravity extremely low in the head. A lower centre of gravity launches the ball higher for a given loft, so the lofts could be strengthened for distance without the ball flight getting too flat.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the 2016 M2?

The 2016 M2 pitching wedge is 43.5 degrees. The set also offers a 49-degree approach wedge, a 55-degree sand wedge and a 60-degree lob wedge.

Does the 2016 M2 iron set include a 3-iron?

No. The stock 2016 M2 set runs from the 4-iron, which is 19 degrees, down through the wedges.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade M2 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
