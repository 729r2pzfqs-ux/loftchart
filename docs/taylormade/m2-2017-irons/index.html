About the TaylorMade M2 (2017)

The second-generation M2 iron was announced on 12 December 2016 and sold as the 2017 model; the year recorded here is the announcement year, following this database's convention. It keeps the distance brief of the 2016 M2 but adds Face Slots, cut near the heel and toe, so that ball speed holds up on strikes away from the centre as well as on the low strikes the Speed Pocket already covered. A six-sided fluted hosel and a 1 mm top line move more weight low, the face is 1.5 mm thin, and a Geocoustic fin badge handles sound. Lofts through the irons are unchanged from the 2016 M2 at 28.5 degrees in the 7-iron, but the lie angles in the 4- and 5-irons are half a degree more upright and the sand and lob wedges were weakened by a degree to 54 and 59. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade 2017 M2 7-iron?

The 2017 TaylorMade M2 7-iron has a loft of 28.5 degrees, with a 63-degree lie and a 37-inch steel-shaft length. That is the same loft as the 2016 M2 7-iron.

What changed between the 2016 M2 irons and the 2017 M2 irons?

The lofts through the irons did not change. The 2017 model adds Face Slots near the heel and toe for forgiveness on strikes away from centre, a six-sided fluted hosel and a 1 mm top line to push weight lower, and a Geocoustic fin badge for sound. The 4- and 5-iron lie angles are half a degree more upright, and the sand and lob wedges are weakened a degree to 54 and 59.

What are Face Slots on the 2017 M2 irons?

Face Slots are narrow slots cut into the face near the heel and the toe. They let those outer parts of the face flex more at impact, which recovers ball speed on heel and toe misses. They complement the Speed Pocket in the sole, which does the same job for strikes low on the face.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the 2017 M2?

The 2017 M2 pitching wedge is 43.5 degrees. The set continues with a 49-degree approach wedge, a 54-degree sand wedge and a 59-degree lob wedge.

Does the 2017 M2 iron set include a 3-iron?

No. The stock set runs from the 4-iron at 19 degrees through the wedges, the same composition as the 2016 M2.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade M2 (2017) ran from 2016 to 2018 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
