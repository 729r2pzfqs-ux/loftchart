About the TaylorMade r7 CGB Max

The r7 CGB Max was the largest and most forgiving iron in TaylorMade's line, announced in October 2007 and at retail from 30 October 2007. It combines an oversize head with toe and heel tungsten weights for very high MOI, a thin high-strength steel face with Inverted Cone Technology, and a rearward centre of gravity to make the ball easy to launch. The SuperFast package pairs it with a light 90-gram steel shaft and a light grip. The 7-iron is 30 degrees and the stock range runs from a 3-iron to a 59-degree lob wedge. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade r7 CGB Max 7-iron?

The TaylorMade r7 CGB Max 7-iron has a loft of 30 degrees, with a 63-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

Are the r7 CGB Max irons strong-lofted?

Yes. The set runs about 2 degrees stronger than the standard r7 through the mid-irons, with a 30-degree 7-iron and a 44-degree pitching wedge, and it uses a compressed 5-iron to 3-iron gap of only 2 degrees each.

Who were the r7 CGB Max irons designed for?

They were TaylorMade's maximum-forgiveness iron, aimed at golfers who want the easiest possible launch. The head is oversize with toe and heel tungsten weights for very high MOI, and the whole club is built light to add clubhead speed.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade r7 CGB Max is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
