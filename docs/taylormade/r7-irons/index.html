About the TaylorMade r7

The r7 iron was TaylorMade's mainstream game-improvement model for 2006, replacing the rac OS at the centre of the range. It uses a deep undercut cavity and perimeter weighting to place the centre of gravity low and back, with a progressively lighter head build and a T-Step 90 steel shaft. The 7-iron is 32 degrees and the stock range runs from a 2-iron to a 60-degree lob wedge. A separate draw-biased r7 Draw model was sold alongside it. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a TaylorMade r7 7-iron?

The TaylorMade r7 7-iron has a loft of 32 degrees, with a 63-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

What year did the TaylorMade r7 irons come out?

The r7 irons arrived in 2006. TaylorMade's own sell sheet lists the right-handed set as available in September 2006 and the left-handed set in October 2006.

What is the difference between the r7 and the r7 Draw?

They share the same lofts, lies and lengths, but the r7 Draw moves weight from the toe toward the heel and uses maximum offset to help the face close through impact. The standard r7 is the neutral-bias version with less offset.

Finding a used set

The TaylorMade r7 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
