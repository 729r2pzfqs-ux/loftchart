About the Cleveland CG4

The CG4 is a mid-size cavity-back aimed at the better player, and the model that established the CG naming that ran through the CG7 and CG16. It is cast from Cleveland's CMM alloy, which the company described as carrying substantially more carbon than ordinary stainless for vibration absorption while being around ten percent less dense, freeing roughly 25 grams to be redistributed low in the head. Lofts are traditional for the period, with a 33.5-degree 7-iron and a 45-degree pitching wedge — the same loft ladder Cleveland carried forward into the CG7. The stock set ran 3-iron through pitching wedge, with a 50-degree DW and a 55-degree SW available to extend it. The numbers below are the standard men's steel-shaft specification.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cleveland CG4 7-iron?

The Cleveland CG4 7-iron has a loft of 33.5 degrees, with a 62.25-degree lie and a 36.75-inch steel-shaft length.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Cleveland CG4?

The CG4 pitching wedge is 45 degrees, roughly 2 degrees weaker than the game-improvement pitching wedges of the same era and 1 degree weaker than a modern set.

How do the Cleveland CG4 lofts compare to the CG7?

They are the same through the set. Both run a 33.5-degree 7-iron and a 45-degree pitching wedge; the CG4 uses slightly flatter lie angles in the mid irons.

Finding a used set

The Cleveland CG4 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
