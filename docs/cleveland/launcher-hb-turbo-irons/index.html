About the Cleveland Launcher HB Turbo

The Launcher HB Turbo is the second-generation hollow-bodied Launcher HB, keeping the fully hollow construction, internal stabilising ribs and HiBore crown while thinning the high-strength steel face for more ball speed. Cleveland added progressive hollow shaping, so the long irons stay hybrid-like while the short irons take on a more conventional iron profile. Lofts are almost unchanged from the Launcher HB, with a 30-degree 7-iron and a 44-degree pitching wedge, but the 6-iron is half a degree stronger, the 8-iron half a degree weaker, the D wedge a degree stronger and the S wedge a degree and a half weaker, and every lie angle was set half a degree more upright than the original. The stock set is 5-iron through D wedge, with the 4-iron and the S wedge sold as individual clubs. Specs below are Cleveland's published steel-shaft numbers; graphite builds share the same lengths and run one swing-weight point lighter.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cleveland Launcher HB Turbo 7-iron?

The Cleveland Launcher HB Turbo 7-iron has a loft of 30 degrees, a lie of 63 degrees and a length of 37 inches.

What are the Cleveland Launcher HB Turbo wedge lofts?

The pitching wedge is 44 degrees, the D (gap) wedge 49 degrees and the S (sand) wedge 54.5 degrees.

How do Launcher HB Turbo lofts compare with the original Launcher HB?

They are nearly identical. The 7-iron and pitching wedge are both unchanged at 30 and 44 degrees; the 6-iron is half a degree stronger at 26.5, the 8-iron half a degree weaker at 34.5, the D wedge is a degree stronger at 49 degrees and the S wedge is a degree and a half weaker at 54.5 degrees. Every lie angle is half a degree more upright.

Which clubs came in a stock Cleveland Launcher HB Turbo set?

The stock set was 5-iron through D wedge. The 4-iron and the S wedge were sold as individual clubs.

Finding a used set

The Cleveland Launcher HB Turbo ran from 2019 to 2022 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
