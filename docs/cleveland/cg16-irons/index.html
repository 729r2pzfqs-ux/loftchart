About the Cleveland CG16

The CG16 is a large cavity-back built around lightweight components and deliberately long club lengths: Cleveland paired ultra-light steel and graphite shafts with optimised head weights so the set could be built longer than normal while still finishing at standard swing weights. The head uses a full undercut cavity to push the centre of gravity low and deep, a large thin face for ball speed, a wide sole, and laser-milled Tour Zip Grooves. Lofts are strengthened compared with the CG7 it replaced — 31 degrees in the 7-iron against 33.5 — and the lengths are unusually long, with a 37.25-inch 7-iron where the CG7 measured 36.75. The stock set ran 3-iron through pitching wedge and was offered in Satin Chrome and Black Pearl finishes, with a separate CG16 Tour model for better players. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cleveland CG16 7-iron?

The Cleveland CG16 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, a lie of 63 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 37.25 inches.

What is the pitching wedge loft of the Cleveland CG16 irons?

The CG16 pitching wedge is 44 degrees, with a 64.5-degree lie and a 35.75-inch steel length.

Why are the Cleveland CG16 irons longer than normal?

Cleveland deliberately built the set long — the 7-iron is 37.25 inches, half an inch longer than the CG7 — and offset the extra length with ultra-lightweight shafts and adjusted head weights so the clubs still finished at a standard swing weight.

Are the Cleveland CG16 lofts stronger than the CG7?

Yes, by around 2 to 2.5 degrees through the mid irons. The CG16 7-iron is 31 degrees against 33.5 on the CG7, and the pitching wedge is 44 degrees against 45.

Finding a used set

The Cleveland CG16 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
