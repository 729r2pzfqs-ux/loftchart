About the Cleveland CG7

The CG7 is the standard, larger-headed member of Cleveland's CG7 family, sold alongside the more compact CG7 Tour. It uses 360-degree Gelback Technology — a one-piece injection-moulded insert that damps vibration across the whole face — together with Dynamic Microcavity Technology, in which the microcavities shorten progressively from the long irons to the short irons to place the centre of gravity correctly in each head, and heel/toe muscle weighting that Cleveland rated at 6 percent higher MOI than a traditional iron. Lofts are traditional rather than strengthened, with a 33.5-degree 7-iron and a 45-degree pitching wedge, and offset is far heavier than on the CG7 Tour at 0.140 inches in the 7-iron against the Tour's 0.115. The stock set ran 3-iron through pitching wedge with a 50-degree DW and a 55-degree SW extending it, and a Black Pearl finish was offered alongside the standard chrome. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cleveland CG7 7-iron?

The Cleveland CG7 7-iron has a loft of 33.5 degrees, a lie of 62.5 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 36.75 inches.

What is the pitching wedge loft of the Cleveland CG7 irons?

The CG7 pitching wedge is 45 degrees, with a 64.5-degree lie and a 35.5-inch steel length. The set extends to a 50-degree DW and a 55-degree SW.

What is the difference between the CG7 and the CG7 Tour?

The standard CG7 is the larger, more forgiving head with much more offset — 0.140 inches at the 7-iron against 0.115 on the Tour — while the CG7 Tour is a compact players head with a Tour sole. The CG7 Tour is also 1.5 degrees stronger through the mid irons, at 34 degrees in the 7-iron versus 33.5.

Finding a used set

The Cleveland CG7 ran from 2009 to 2010 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
