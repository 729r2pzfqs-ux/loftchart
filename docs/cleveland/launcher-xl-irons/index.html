About the Cleveland Launcher XL

The Launcher XL is an oversize distance iron built as a progressive set: the long irons are hollow bodied for forgiveness, while the short irons switch to a conventional cavity back for control. Cleveland pairs that with an enlarged head and its MainFrame face milling, and quotes an MOI of 3,081 g-cm2 in the 7-iron. Lofts are very strong, with a 29-degree 7-iron and a 43-degree pitching wedge, so the set is roughly a club stronger than a traditional iron at the same number. The stock set runs 4-iron through pitching wedge with a 48-degree DW gap wedge extending it. Cleveland also offers an Accuracy Build, which shortens every club by half an inch and drops one swing-weight point; the specs below are the standard build with steel shafts.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cleveland Launcher XL 7-iron?

The Cleveland Launcher XL 7-iron has a loft of 29 degrees, a lie of 63 degrees and a standard build length of 37 inches.

What is the pitching wedge loft of the Cleveland Launcher XL irons?

The Launcher XL pitching wedge is 43 degrees, with a 64.5-degree lie and a 35.75-inch length. A 48-degree DW gap wedge extends the set.

What is the Cleveland Launcher XL Accuracy Build?

It is an alternative stock build in which every club is half an inch shorter than standard — a 36.5-inch 7-iron instead of 37 inches — and one swing-weight point lighter, aimed at golfers who want more control than the standard long build gives.

Finding a used set

The Cleveland Launcher XL is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
