About the Cleveland CBX

The CBX, sold as the Launcher CBX, is the cavity-back half of the Launcher iron range Cleveland introduced in 2017, positioned between the hollow-bodied Launcher HB and a players iron. The 4 through 7 use a Launcher Face Cup that wraps around the chassis so the weld line sits behind the face, preserving ball speed on off-centre strikes, while the short irons move to a more conventional cavity for control. Lofts are strong for a cavity back at 30 degrees in the 7-iron and 44 degrees in the pitching wedge, and the set carries a moderate amount of offset, tapering from about 4.2 mm in the 4-iron to 1.8 mm in the gap wedge. The stock set ran 4-iron through pitching wedge with a 50-degree D gap wedge available to extend it. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cleveland CBX 7-iron?

The Cleveland CBX 7-iron has a loft of 30 degrees, a lie of 62.5 degrees and a length of 37 inches.

What is the pitching wedge loft of the Cleveland CBX irons?

The CBX pitching wedge is 44 degrees, with a 64-degree lie and a 35.75-inch length. A 50-degree D wedge extends the set.

How does the Cleveland CBX differ from the Launcher HB?

The CBX is a cavity back with a face cup in the long and mid irons, aimed at players who still want some workability. The Launcher HB is fully hollow through the whole set and wider soled. From the 7-iron down their lofts are identical — 30 degrees at the 7-iron and 44 at the pitching wedge — and the CBX long irons are 1 degree stronger, with a 20-degree 4-iron against the HB's 21.

Finding a used set

The Cleveland CBX is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
