About the Cleveland Launcher

The Launcher irons are an oversize game-improvement set built around a split design: the 3, 4 and 5 are hybrid-style heads with the largest face in the set and a parabolic leading edge, while the 6 through sand wedge are conventional oversize iron heads with a thick topline and extra offset. Both halves use Cleveland's extended horseshoe perimeter weighting, which the company rated at 20 percent higher MOI than a traditional iron and up to 3,000 g-cm2 in the long irons. Lofts are moderately strong for the period at 33 degrees in the 7-iron and 45 degrees in the pitching wedge. The stock set ran 3-iron through sand wedge, including a 50-degree DW gap wedge and a 55-degree SW. The specs below are the standard men's steel-shaft numbers from Cleveland's own product page; graphite builds were a quarter inch longer at every club.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Cleveland Launcher 7-iron?

The Cleveland Launcher 7-iron has a loft of 33 degrees, a lie of 62.5 degrees and a steel-shaft length of 37 inches.

What are the Cleveland Launcher wedge lofts?

The pitching wedge is 45 degrees, the DW gap wedge 50 degrees and the sand wedge 55 degrees.

Are the Cleveland Launcher long irons really hybrids?

The 3, 4 and 5 are hybrid-style heads built on an iron chassis with the largest face in the set and a parabolic leading edge, while the 6-iron through sand wedge are conventional oversize iron heads.

Finding a used set

The Cleveland Launcher ran from 2005 to 2010 and is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
