About the Callaway Big Bertha X-12

The Big Bertha X-12 arrived in 1998 as Callaway's first multi-piece iron, using a 360 degree undercut channel to push weight low and to the perimeter. It carries the same loft and lie ladder as the original Big Bertha irons, with a 33 degree 7-iron and a 46.5 degree pitching wedge, and the same upright 65 degree lie through the wedges. Only the approach wedge changed, moving from 50.8 to 51 degrees. It became one of the best-selling iron sets of its decade and directly preceded the Steelhead X-14.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway X-12 7-iron?

The Callaway Big Bertha X-12 7-iron is 33 degrees, with a 62.5 degree lie and a 37 inch standard length.

Do the Callaway X-12 and the 1994 Big Bertha share the same lofts?

Almost exactly. Both run a 33 degree 7-iron and a 46.5 degree pitching wedge on an identical lie and length ladder; only the approach wedge differs, at 51 degrees against 50.8.

What shafts came in the Callaway X-12?

The stock steel shaft was the Memphis 10 '98, with the RCH Series 99 graphite offered in Light, Regular, Firm and Strong flexes.

Finding a used set

The Callaway Big Bertha X-12 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
