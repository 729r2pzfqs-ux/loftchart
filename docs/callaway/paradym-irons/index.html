About the Callaway Paradym

Released in February 2023, the Paradym is a hollow-bodied cast iron built on what Callaway calls Speed Frame construction, with an A.I.-designed forged 455 steel face cup, up to 67 grams of tungsten to set the centre of gravity low and patented urethane microspheres behind the face. Lofts sit between the Rogue ST Max it followed and the more distance-oriented Paradym X sold alongside it, with a 29 degree 7-iron and a 42 degree pitching wedge. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway Paradym 7-iron?

The Callaway Paradym 7-iron has a loft of 29 degrees, with a 62.5 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the loft of a Callaway Paradym pitching wedge?

The Callaway Paradym pitching wedge is 42 degrees, with a 47 degree A-wedge and a 52 degree gap wedge.

What is the difference between the Paradym and Paradym X irons?

The Paradym X is the stronger-lofted, more forgiving model. Its 7-iron is 27.5 degrees against 29 for the standard Paradym, and its pitching wedge is 41 degrees against 42, with more offset and more tungsten.

Are the Callaway Paradym irons forged?

The head is cast, but the face is a forged 455 steel cup designed using artificial intelligence, set into a hollow body with internal tungsten weighting and urethane microspheres.

Finding a used set

The Callaway Paradym is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
