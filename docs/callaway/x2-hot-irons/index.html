About the Callaway X2 Hot

The X2 Hot is the second-generation X Hot game-improvement iron, replacing the 2013 model with a Deep Central Undercut Cavity and an internal Stabilizing Arch that let Callaway thin the centre of the face further for ball speed. Callaway also added roughly two to four degrees more bounce than the X Hot to improve turf interaction, and progressed the offset so the short irons sit straighter than the long irons. The loft structure is carried over unchanged from the X Hot at 30 degrees in the 7-iron and 44 degrees in the pitching wedge; the differences are in the long irons, where the 4-, 5- and 6-iron are a quarter of a degree more upright and around a quarter to three-eighths of an inch shorter, and the 3-iron is a quarter of a degree flatter. From the 7-iron down the lie and length figures are identical to the X Hot. Stock sets ran 3-PW, 4-PW, 5-PW or 6-PW with A, S and L wedges sold as add-ons; a smaller, weaker-lofted X2 Hot Pro was sold alongside it. Callaway announced the line on 12 November 2013 and it reached retail on 17 January 2014, so it is widely known as the 2014 X2 Hot even though the year below is the announcement year per this database's convention.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway X2 Hot 7-iron?

The Callaway X2 Hot 7-iron has a loft of 30 degrees, a lie of 62.5 degrees and a length of 37 inches.

What are the Callaway X2 Hot wedge lofts?

The pitching wedge is 44 degrees, the A-wedge 49 degrees, the sand wedge 54 degrees and the lob wedge 59 degrees.

Are Callaway X2 Hot irons stronger lofted than the X Hot?

No. The X2 Hot carries the same loft structure as the 2013 X Hot from the 3-iron through the lob wedge. The changes are in bounce, offset and the flatter, shorter long irons.

What year are Callaway X2 Hot irons?

Callaway announced them on 12 November 2013 and they went on sale on 17 January 2014, which is why they are usually referred to as the 2014 X2 Hot.

Finding a used set

The Callaway X2 Hot is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
