About the Callaway X Hot

The 2013 X Hot revived the X name for Callaway's distance-oriented game-improvement iron, taking over from the RAZR X family. It used a deep undercut cavity with a thin, unsupported face section to raise ball speed, and Callaway lowered the sweet spot roughly 0.155 inches versus the previous RAZR X game-improvement head. Lofts were notably strong for the period, with a 30 degree 7-iron and a 44 degree pitching wedge. The stock set was 4-PW with an A-wedge, and 3-iron, sand wedge and lob wedge were offered separately. A smaller X Hot Pro model with weaker lofts was sold alongside it.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway X Hot 7-iron?

The 2013 Callaway X Hot 7-iron has a loft of 30 degrees.

What is the loft of a Callaway X Hot pitching wedge?

The Callaway X Hot pitching wedge is 44 degrees, with a 49 degree A-wedge and a 54 degree sand wedge.

How long is a standard Callaway X Hot 7-iron?

The standard Callaway X Hot 7-iron is 37 inches with a 62.5 degree lie angle.

Finding a used set

The Callaway X Hot is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
