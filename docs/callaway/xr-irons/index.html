About the Callaway XR

The XR was Callaway's 2015 game-improvement iron, replacing the X2 Hot. It uses a two-piece cast head with dual heat treatment, allowing a thinner 360 Face Cup that wraps around the perimeter of the face, together with an Internal Standing Wave that positions mass low and central. This generation predates the tungsten weighting and urethane microspheres of later Callaway irons. Lofts are strong for the period, with a 30 degree 7-iron and a 44 degree pitching wedge, and the published range runs from a 3-iron to a lob wedge. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway XR 7-iron?

The Callaway XR 7-iron has a loft of 30 degrees, with a 62.5 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the loft of a Callaway XR pitching wedge?

The Callaway XR pitching wedge is 44 degrees, with a 49 degree A-wedge, a 54 degree sand wedge and a 59 degree lob wedge.

What is the difference between the Callaway XR and the Steelhead XR?

The lofts, lies and lengths are almost identical, differing only in the 3-iron lie angle. The heads are genuinely different though: the Steelhead XR has substantially less offset, a hollow bore-through hosel and a steel-infused polyurethane layer behind the face.

Finding a used set

The Callaway XR is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
