About the Callaway Rogue

Released in February 2018, the Rogue was Callaway's mainstream game-improvement iron between the Steelhead XR and the Mavrik. It pairs a 360 Face Cup with Variable Face Thickness so the face flexes across a wider area, adds tungsten weighting to lower the centre of gravity, and fills the head with elastic urethane microspheres to absorb vibration without restricting face speed. Note that the Rogue predates Callaway's artificial-intelligence face design, which reached the iron line with the Mavrik in 2020. The published set is unusually deep, running from a 3-iron to a lob wedge. Specs below are the standard men's steel-shaft numbers.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway Rogue 7-iron?

The Callaway Rogue 7-iron has a loft of 30 degrees, with a 62.5 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the loft of a Callaway Rogue pitching wedge?

The Callaway Rogue pitching wedge is 44 degrees, with a 49 degree A-wedge, a 54 degree sand wedge and a 59 degree lob wedge.

Do the Callaway Rogue irons have an A.I.-designed face?

No. The 2018 Rogue uses a 360 Face Cup with Variable Face Thickness. Callaway's artificial-intelligence face design first appeared in the Epic Flash driver in 2019 and did not reach the iron line until the Mavrik in 2020.

How long is a standard Callaway Rogue 7-iron?

The standard men's steel-shaft Callaway Rogue 7-iron is 37 inches with a 62.5 degree lie angle.

Finding a used set

The Callaway Rogue is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
