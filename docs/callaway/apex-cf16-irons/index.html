About the Callaway Apex CF16

Announced in September 2015 for the 2016 model year, the Apex CF16 replaced the original 2014 Apex as Callaway's forged players-distance iron. The body is quadruple-net forged from 1025 mild carbon steel including the hosel, with a laser-welded forged cup face. The 360 Face Cup appears only in the 3 through 7 irons; the 8-iron through A-wedge use an engineered face plate instead. Lofts, lies and lengths carry over unchanged from the 2014 Apex, with a 31 degree 7-iron and a 45 degree pitching wedge. Specs below are the standard men's steel-shaft numbers.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway Apex CF16 7-iron?

The Callaway Apex CF16 7-iron has a loft of 31 degrees, with a 62 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the loft of a Callaway Apex CF16 pitching wedge?

The Apex CF16 pitching wedge is 45 degrees, with a 50 degree A-wedge and a 55 degree sand wedge.

Are the Apex CF16 lofts the same as the 2014 Apex?

Yes. The Apex CF16 carries over the 2014 Apex loft, lie and length chart unchanged, from a 19 degree 3-iron to a 55 degree sand wedge. The differences are in construction, not specification.

Do all the Apex CF16 irons have the 360 Face Cup?

No. The 360 Face Cup is used in the 3 through 7 irons only. The 8-iron, 9-iron, pitching wedge and A-wedge use an engineered face plate instead.

Buying a set

Callaway has since introduced the Callaway Apex 19 above it in the range, so the Apex CF16 is at the end of its retail life rather than fully retired — closeout sets still turn up at retailers while stock lasts, and it is well represented used on eBay, 2nd Swing and Golf Avenue. Check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying a used set: irons are routinely bent from standard during a fitting.
