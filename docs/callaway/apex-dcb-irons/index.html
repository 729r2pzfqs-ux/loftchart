About the Callaway Apex DCB

The Apex DCB launched alongside the Apex 21 as the forgiving member of the 2021 Apex family. DCB stands for deep cavity back: the head uses a deeper dual cavity and a wider sole than the Apex 21, moving the centre of gravity lower and further back for an easier launch. Construction is otherwise shared with the Apex 21, a forged 1025 mild carbon steel body with an A.I.-designed Flash Face Cup, tungsten in the long and mid irons and urethane microspheres. Lofts are half a degree weaker than the Apex 21 at the 7-iron and the set is more upright through the short irons. Specs below are the standard men's steel-shaft numbers.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway Apex DCB 7-iron?

The Callaway Apex DCB 7-iron has a loft of 30 degrees, with a 62.5 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the loft of a Callaway Apex DCB pitching wedge?

The Apex DCB pitching wedge is 43 degrees and the matching A-wedge is 48 degrees, the same as the Apex 21.

What is the difference between the Apex DCB and the Apex 21?

The DCB uses a deeper dual cavity back and a wider sole for a lower, deeper centre of gravity and easier launch. Its 7-iron is 30 degrees against 30.5 for the Apex 21, and its short irons are half a degree to one degree more upright.

What does DCB stand for on the Callaway Apex irons?

DCB stands for deep cavity back, referring to the deeper dual cavity construction that makes it the more forgiving model in the 2021 Apex family.

Finding a used set

The Callaway Apex DCB is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
