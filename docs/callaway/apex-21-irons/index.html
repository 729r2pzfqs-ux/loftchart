About the Callaway Apex 21

The Apex 21 is a 100 percent forged 1025 mild carbon steel iron carrying an A.I.-designed Flash Face Cup, a Tungsten Energy Core to set the centre of gravity club by club, and urethane microspheres for feel. Lofts are unchanged from the Apex 19 everywhere except the 4-iron, holding a 30.5 degree 7-iron and a 43 degree pitching wedge. Published spec tables run 3-iron to A-wedge with no sand wedge in the set. Specs below are the standard men's steel-shaft numbers.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway Apex 21 7-iron?

The Callaway Apex 21 7-iron has a loft of 30.5 degrees, with a 62 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the loft of a Callaway Apex 21 pitching wedge?

The Apex 21 pitching wedge is 43 degrees and the matching A-wedge is 48 degrees.

What is the difference between the Apex 21 and the Apex 19 lofts?

They are identical apart from the 4-iron. Both sets run a 30.5 degree 7-iron and a 43 degree pitching wedge; the change from Apex 19 to Apex 21 is in construction, principally the move to an A.I.-designed Flash Face Cup.

Are the Callaway Apex 21 irons fully forged?

Yes. The Apex 21 body is 100 percent forged from 1025 mild carbon steel, with a forged Flash Face Cup and an internal tungsten energy core.

Finding a used set

The Callaway Apex 21 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
