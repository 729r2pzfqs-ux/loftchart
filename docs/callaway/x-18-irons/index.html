About the Callaway X-18

The X-18 replaced the X-16 in 2004 and moved the line back toward slightly weaker lofts, with a 33 degree 7-iron against the X-16's 34 and a 41 degree 9-iron against 42. It keeps the upright Callaway lie ladder topping out at 65 degrees through the wedges and the notched, undercut cavity that defined the mid-2000s X-series. Callaway listed the stock steel build as Constant Weight Uniflex Steel, and published no bounce column for this generation. The catalogued range ran 2-iron to lob wedge.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway X-18 7-iron?

The Callaway X-18 7-iron is 33 degrees, with a 62.5 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

Is the Callaway X-18 stronger or weaker than the X-16?

Weaker through the middle and short irons. The X-18 7-iron is 33 degrees against 34 on the X-16, and its 9-iron is 41 degrees against 42.

What is the pitching wedge loft on the Callaway X-18?

The X-18 pitching wedge is 46 degrees, with a 51 degree approach wedge, a 56 degree sand wedge and a 60 degree lob wedge.

Finding a used set

The Callaway X-18 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
