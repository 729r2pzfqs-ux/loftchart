About the Callaway X-22

The X-22 followed the X-20 in 2008 and was the last numbered X-series iron to sell in real volume before Callaway moved the line toward the X Hot and XR names. It uses a notched, undercut cavity with weight pushed to the extreme heel and toe for a higher moment of inertia than the X-20, and keeps the S2H2 hosel. Contrary to the common assumption that each generation grew stronger, Callaway's own spec sheet shows the X-22 carrying the X-20 loft ladder essentially unchanged, with a 33 degree 7-iron and a 45 degree pitching wedge. A smaller X-22 Tour model with more traditional lofts was sold alongside it.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway X-22 7-iron?

The Callaway X-22 7-iron is 33 degrees, with a 62.5 degree lie and a 36.75 inch standard steel length.

Is the Callaway X-22 stronger lofted than the X-20?

No. Callaway's published spec sheet gives the X-22 the same 33 degree 7-iron and 45 degree pitching wedge as the X-20. Secondary sources listing it a degree stronger are not supported by the manufacturer table.

What is the difference between the X-22 and the X-22 Tour?

The X-22 Tour has a smaller head, a thinner topline, less offset and more traditional lofts, aimed at better players. The standard X-22 listed here is the larger game improvement model.

Finding a used set

The Callaway X-22 is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
