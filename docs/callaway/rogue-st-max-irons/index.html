About the Callaway Rogue ST Max

The Rogue ST Max was the core game-improvement iron of Callaway's 2022 Rogue ST family, which revived the Rogue name four years after the original. The head is cast from high-strength 450 stainless steel around an A.I.-designed Flash Face Cup, with up to 62 grams of high-density tungsten placed low in the head and patented urethane microspheres behind the face. Lofts sit half a degree weaker than the Mavrik at the 7-iron but match it at the pitching wedge. Specs below are the standard men's steel-shaft numbers.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway Rogue ST Max 7-iron?

The Callaway Rogue ST Max 7-iron has a loft of 27.5 degrees, with a 62.5 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the loft of a Callaway Rogue ST Max pitching wedge?

The Rogue ST Max pitching wedge is 41 degrees, with a 46 degree A-wedge, a 51 degree gap wedge and a 56 degree sand wedge.

How do the Rogue ST Max lofts compare with the Mavrik?

They are very close. The Rogue ST Max 7-iron is 27.5 degrees against 27 for the Mavrik, and both sets share a 41 degree pitching wedge, a 46 degree A-wedge and a 56 degree sand wedge.

Is the Rogue ST Max the same as the 2018 Callaway Rogue?

No. They are separate models four years apart. The 2018 Rogue uses a 360 Face Cup and has a 30 degree 7-iron, while the 2022 Rogue ST Max uses an A.I.-designed Flash Face Cup in high-strength 450 stainless steel and has a 27.5 degree 7-iron.

Finding a used set

The Callaway Rogue ST Max is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
