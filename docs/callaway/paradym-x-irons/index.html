About the Callaway Paradym X

The Paradym X is the distance and forgiveness model of Callaway's 2023 Paradym iron family. It shares the hollow-body Speed Frame construction and A.I.-designed forged 455 face cup of the standard Paradym but carries more tungsten, up to 79 grams against 67, along with a larger profile and more offset. Lofts are a degree and a half stronger at the 7-iron, 27.5 degrees against 29, and the published range extends to a sand wedge. Specs below are the standard men's steel-shaft numbers.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway Paradym X 7-iron?

The Callaway Paradym X 7-iron has a loft of 27.5 degrees, with a 62.5 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the loft of a Callaway Paradym X pitching wedge?

The Paradym X pitching wedge is 41 degrees, with a 46 degree A-wedge, a 51 degree gap wedge and a 56 degree sand wedge.

Is the Paradym X stronger lofted than the standard Paradym?

Yes. The Paradym X 7-iron is 27.5 degrees against 29 for the standard Paradym, and the difference runs about one to one and a half degrees through the set.

Which 2023 Callaway Paradym iron is more forgiving?

The Paradym X. It uses a larger head, more offset and up to 79 grams of tungsten against the standard Paradym's 67 grams, placing the centre of gravity lower and deeper.

Finding a used set

The Callaway Paradym X is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
