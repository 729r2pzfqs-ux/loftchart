About the Callaway XR OS

The XR OS is the oversize, maximum-forgiveness version of Callaway's XR iron, announced on 12 January 2016 and at retail from 22 January. OS stands for oversize: the head is larger than the standard XR with a wider sole and a lower, deeper centre of gravity to raise launch. It also runs longer than the standard XR through the mid irons, with a 37.25 inch 7-iron against 37 inches. Lofts are weaker than the standard XR in the long and mid irons, a 31.5 degree 7-iron against 30, but converge from the 9-iron down. Specs below are the standard men's steel-shaft numbers.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway XR OS 7-iron?

The Callaway XR OS 7-iron has a loft of 31.5 degrees, with a 62.5 degree lie and a 37.25 inch standard steel length.

What is the loft of a Callaway XR OS pitching wedge?

The XR OS pitching wedge is 44 degrees, with a 49 degree A-wedge and a 54 degree sand wedge, matching the standard XR.

What does OS mean on the Callaway XR OS irons?

OS stands for oversize. The XR OS has a larger head, a wider sole and a lower, deeper centre of gravity than the standard XR, making it the more forgiving and higher-launching of the two.

Are the XR OS irons from 2015 or 2016?

2016. Callaway announced the XR OS irons on 12 January 2016 with retail availability from 22 January 2016, a year after the standard XR.

Finding a used set

The Callaway XR OS is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
