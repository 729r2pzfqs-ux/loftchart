About the Callaway Mavrik

Launched in January 2020, the Mavrik was the first Callaway iron designed with the company's artificial-intelligence face software, which produced a Flash Face Cup shaped individually for every loft in the set rather than one face scaled across it. A Tungsten Energy Core positions the centre of gravity club by club and urethane microspheres damp vibration. Lofts are strong for the category, with a 27 degree 7-iron and a 41 degree pitching wedge. Specs below are the standard men's steel-shaft numbers.

Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway Mavrik 7-iron?

The Callaway Mavrik 7-iron has a loft of 27 degrees, with a 62.5 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the loft of a Callaway Mavrik pitching wedge?

The Callaway Mavrik pitching wedge is 41 degrees, with a 46 degree A-wedge, a 51 degree gap wedge and a 56 degree sand wedge.

Are the Callaway Mavrik irons stronger lofted than the Rogue?

Yes, by three degrees through most of the set. The Mavrik 7-iron is 27 degrees against 30 for the Rogue, and the pitching wedge is 41 degrees against 44.

What makes the Mavrik iron face different?

The Mavrik was the first Callaway iron whose face was designed by artificial intelligence, producing a Flash Face Cup shaped uniquely for each loft in the set instead of a single face design scaled across every club.

Finding a used set

The Callaway Mavrik is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
