About the Callaway Steelhead XR

In stores from 2 September 2016, the Steelhead XR revived the Steelhead name Callaway had used on the X-14 era irons. It combines a next-generation 360 Face Cup with a hollow bore-through hosel and a steel-infused polyurethane layer behind the face, the precursor to the urethane microspheres used in later Callaway irons. Lofts, lies and lengths carry over from the standard XR almost exactly, but offset is cut substantially, from 0.220 inches at the 3-iron against the XR's 0.270. Frequently asked questions

What is the loft of a Callaway Steelhead XR 7-iron?

The Callaway Steelhead XR 7-iron has a loft of 30 degrees, with a 62.5 degree lie and a 37 inch standard steel length.

What is the loft of a Callaway Steelhead XR pitching wedge?

The Steelhead XR pitching wedge is 44 degrees, with a 49 degree A-wedge, a 54 degree sand wedge and a 59 degree lob wedge.

How do the Steelhead XR and XR irons differ?

The specifications are nearly identical, differing only in the 3-iron lie angle. The Steelhead XR is a different head, with markedly less offset, a hollow bore-through hosel and a steel-infused polyurethane layer behind the face.

Is the Steelhead XR related to the older Callaway Steelhead irons?

Only by name. The Steelhead XR revived a badge Callaway last used on the X-14 era irons around 2000, but it is a 2016 design built on the XR platform rather than a continuation of that head.

Finding a used set

The Callaway Steelhead XR is no longer in production, so the used market is the only source. Search eBay, 2nd Swing, Golf Avenue and the PGA Tour Superstore trade-in listings, and check the loft and lie against the chart above before buying — sets this age have often been bent from standard.
